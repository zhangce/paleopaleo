LITHOSTRATIGRAPHY

The Grassy Spring Member is proposed here to replace the informal A-shale member designation by Merriam (1964), al- though a somewhat higher stratigraphic horizon is selected as the base. The type area for this member is in the S% 1/4 Sec.

11, TSS, R64E, two kilometers south of Grassy Spring, Pahroc Spring SE 7.5' quadrangle, Lincoln County, Nevada. The Grassy Spring Member is the highest unit in the Pioche Shale, and can be recognized from the Delamar Mountains northward to the Bristol Range.

The Grassy Spring Member is 100 meters thick, and is com- posed predominantly (80 percent) of clastics, with distinct car- bonate horizons. The clastics include fissile shale, bioturbated silty Inudstone, and quartz sandstone. The carbonates include oolitic, oncolitic, bioclastic, and silty limestones. The thicker bedded carbonates are restricted to the middle third of the mem- ber. The "basal sandstone marker" of Merriam (1964) is not included in this member, as it is part of a coarsening upward cycle of the underlying 8-shale member.
