Discussion.â€” This species is unique in that it is the only spe- cies of AIbertella with posteriorly directed marginal spines. This is the only pygidial feature that does not fit Palmer's (in Palmer and Halley, 1979) generic description. Cranidial characteristics of A. highlandensis do, however, compare quite closely to the generic description. Cranidia of A. highlandensis are similar in many respects to cranidia of Mexicaspis stenopyge Lochman (1948) and M. difunrosensis Lochman (in Cooper et al., 1952), but differ by having much shorter and narrower palpebral lobes and an occipital furrow that is uniform in depth and width.
