Volocephalina variosa can be distinguished from V. connexa by having an eye ridge that terminates at the axial furrow, narrower fixigena, and a shorter posterior area of the fixigena. Volocephalina contracta Palmer (in Palmer and Halley, 1979) does have an eye ridge that terminates at the axial furrow, but it has a shorter frontal area, narrower fixigena, and a slightly shorter posterior area of the fixigena than V. variosa. Even though the morphologic differences between V. variosa and V. contracta are slight, V. variosa occurs in beds that are stratigraphically lower than those of the Cararra Formation where the type material of V. contracta was described.
