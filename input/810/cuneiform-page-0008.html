The Albertella highlandensis Subbiozone occurs in the upper 30 meters of the Pioche Shale. Trilobites restricted to this sub- biozone include A. highlandensis new species, Plagiura minor Palmer (in Palmer and Halley, 1979), and VoloceIphalina con- nexa Palmer (in Palmer and Halley, 1979). A/bertella schenki Resser (in McKee and Resser, 1945) and an Olenoides species occur in fissile shales inn>ediately adjacent to the Albertella highlandensis shell beds in the Pioche Hills. Faunal elements of this subbiozone can be traced from the Death Valley region (Palmer and Halley, 1979) to the Grand Canyon (McKee and Resser, 1945).

SYSTEMATIC PALEONTOLOGY

All of the trilobite faunas described in this study were col- lected from the Albertella Biozone within the Grassy Spring Member of the Pioche Shale of southeastern Nevada. Sections are located in the Delamar Mountains, Chief Range, Highland Range, and the Pioche Hills (also known as the Ely Range). Illustrated specimens and the associated fauna are deposited in the United States National Museum (USNM). Each faunal ho- rizon within a measured section is given its own locality number, for a total of 64 USNM locality numbers. USNM specimen num- bers have been assigned only to type and redescribed specimens. Detailed locality data is presented in the appendix, and measured sections can be obtained from L. B. McCollum.
