2.1.1.2. Ghost Creek Formation. The Ghost Creek Formation is a recessive unit exposed along shorelines and in creeks and road cuts on central Graham Island, Skidegate and Cumshewa inlets, Louise Island and I<unga Island. The formation is up to 65 m thick, composed of dark grey shale and silty shale with occasional pale grey limestone interbeds and lenses that yield well-preserved radiolarians. It differs from the underlying Sandilands Formation in having more shale and much less volcanic material and by lacl<ing laminated bedding (Cameron and Tipper, 1985).
