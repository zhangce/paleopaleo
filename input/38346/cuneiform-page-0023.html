Nine zones are defined: four Early Pliensbachian, one Late Pliensbachian, one Early Toarcian, one Middle-Late Toarcian, and two Aalenian (Fig. 4). Each zone contains one or more UA and each UA is defined by co-existing species pairs and by the totality of its co- occurring species. Thus a sample can be assigned to a zone if one or more of its UA can be identified. Because of some distinct differences between middle to high latitude faunas of more northerly areas and low-latitude faunas of Tethys, generally one zonal name has been chosen from QCI and the other from Tethys. Nominal and a few other stratigraphically important species are presented in Plate T.

Because independent dating is available only for faunas from Queen Charlotte Islands, six ammonite zones (and several ages in the case of the Toarcian and Aalenian) were incorporated into the zonation to help constrain the biochronology.
