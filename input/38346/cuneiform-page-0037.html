The new zonation presented herein now allows global zonation for radiolarians across the entire Jurassic System.

And finally we note that the Lower Pliensbachian has yielded four radiolarian zones that correlate with an equal number of ammonite assemblages/zones for this same interval of time. This leads us to believe that in closely sampled, continuous sequences with no evidence of tectonics, radiolarians potentially may equal the precision of ammonites for biochronologically dating the Jurassic.

The proposed zonation integrates virtually all available data on the global distribution of Pliensbachian and Toarcian radiolarians. As such, it also provides a solid basis upon which radiolarian faunal turnovers through the critical Pliensbachian-Toarcian interval can be analyzed. Moreover, the collected data include occurrences in low and middle to high paleolatitudes, that will allow further research into paleobiogeography of Jurassic radiolarians.
