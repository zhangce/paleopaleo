2.1.2.1. Whiteaves Formation. The Whiteaves Formation is a pale grey- green weathering poorly exposed shale unit that rests conformably on the Fannin Formation. The formation is exposed in Sl<idegate Inlet and central Graham Island (thicl<ening to the north) and depending on the location spans most of the Lower to Upper Toarcian. The collection from section 12 on Maude Island (GSC loc. C-080579) is associated with a specimen of Phymotoceras (Carter et al., 1988, p. 16) belonging to the Middle Toarcian Planulata Zone of Jal<obs (1997), which corresponds to the NW European Bifrons Zone.

2.1.2.2. Phantom Creek Formation. The Phantom Creek Formation is a resistant unit comprised mainly of brown- to buff-weathering, partly calcareous, fine- to coarse-grained sandstone resting conformably on the Whiteaves Formation. Section 13 contains four radiolarian collections: C-080583 and C-080584 are associated with ammonites of late Middle-early Late Toarcian age, C-080597 with ammonites of the Late Toarcian Yal<ounensis Zone (corresponding to the NW European Levesquei Zone), and C-156399 with ammonites of early Aalenian age. Sample C-080586 (section 14) is associated with the early Aalenian ammonite Tmetoceras (Carter et al., 1988); see also Carter and Jakobs (1991) and Poulton and Tipper (1991) for further information on the early Aalenian radiolarian and ammonite faunas.

2.1.2.3. Graham Island Formation. Radiolarians from the lower part of the Formation are associated with ammonites of the Early Bajocian North American Widebayense Zone (Hall and Westermann, 1980), which corresponds broadly to the Discites and Ovalis zones of Northwest Europe. See Carter et al. (1988) for further details.
