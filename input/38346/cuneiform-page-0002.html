To address this need, a joint international project was initiated under the frameworl< of INTERRAD (International Association of Radiolarian Paleontologists) during the 9th INTERRAD meeting in Blairsden, California in 2000. The first step for the Pliensbachian to Aalenian Worl<ing Group (PTA) was to establish a set of clearly defined and updated taxonomic species that would be used in the construction of the zonation and to agree upon the sections to be used. This was accomplished during meetings in 2001 and 2002 in Slovenia, and final editing in 2004 and 2005. The 'Catalogue and systematics of Pliensbachian, Toarcian and Aalenian radiolarian genera and species' is now complete (Gorican et al., 2006).

The systematics of 280 taxa is included, each with an up-to-date synonymy, original and subsequent definitions, remarks, and data on geographic distribution. The plates illustrate the holotype and sufficient other specimens from different paleogeographic realms to clearly illustrate intraspecific variability. Concurrently, a preliminary zonation calculated with the UA program BioGraph (Savary and Guex, 1991, 1999) was presented at the 6th International Symposium of the Jurassic System in Sicily (September 2002, Palermo) and the 10th meeting of INTERRAD (September 2003, Lausanne). The species in this catalogue provide the framework upon which zonation is constructed. Species to be included in the total dataset were selected conserva- tively: indeed, the ranges of fewer than half the available species were incorporated in the final dataset. Eliminated were those that are either rare, long ranging or non-diagnostic with wide limits of variability, or others that occur sporadically because their skeletons are too delicate to be preserved. A list of all species, with generic names updated according to the recent revision of 0 Dogherty et al. (2009), is given in Appendix A (see supplement data in the electronic version).
