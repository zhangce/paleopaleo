2.3. East-central Oregon

Yeh (1987a,b) described radiolarians from limestone concretions in the Nicely and Hyde formations in east-central Oregon. These excellently preserved faunas are from disparate localities and dating is confirmed mainly by superposition. The Nicely Formation contains diverse Pliensbachian ammonite faunas ranging as high as the 1<unae Zone (early Late Pliensbachian) (Imlay, 1968; Smith et

al., 1988) and is overlain conformably by the volcaniclastic Hyde Formation, which contains no diagnostic macrofossils. Basal ammonite assemblages of the overlying Warm Springs Member of the Snowshoe Formation belong to the highest zone of the Toarcian, the Yal<ounensis Zone (Jal<obs et al., 1994; Jal<obs and Smith, 1996). Thus, the Hyde Formation could range from the lower part of the Upper Pliensbachian to the uppermost Toarcian. Radiolarian faunas from the Hyde Formation differ significantly from known Late Pliensbachian and Middle Toarcian ones and are generally believed to be intermediate between the two, hence Early Toarcian. Nagai (1990) later reported on additional radiolarians from the Hyde Formation.
