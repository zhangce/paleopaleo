The existing radiolarian zonations for the Pliensbachian to Aalenian time interval (Pessagno et aL, 1987; Carter et aL, 1988; Hori, 1990) are local and mainly characterized by a low- resolution potential. The only high-resolution range chart is the Late Pliensbachian to Early Bajocian zonation constructed for Queen Charlotte Islands (Carter et al., 1988) but it contains an unfossiliferous interval in the Early Toarcian.

The primary goal of this paper is to establish a precise radiolarian range chart that will serve as a powerful tool for dating Pliensbachian to Aalenian pelagic successions on a global scale. We present biostratigraphic ranges of 197 species in 220 samples from various paleogeographic provinces that include low and middle to high paleolatitudes. The zonation will have important implications for accurate dating around one of the major ecologic and biotic crisis that culminated in the Early Toarcian. In further radiolarian research the zonation can serve as a basis for studying radiolarian faunal turnovers through this critical interval, similar to the manner in which zonations by Jud (1994) and O'Dogherty (1994) were used to analyze radiolarian turnovers through the Cretaceous (O'Dogherty and Guex, 2002). In addition, the vast dataset provides new insights into the paleobiogeography of Jurassic radiolarians.
