The Unitary Association (UA) method (Guex, 1977) is used herein to integrate the large quantity of data presented into a global radiolarian biochronology for the Pliensbachian, Toarcian and Aalenian. The objectives and procedures of this method are described in detail in Guex (1991) and are not repeated here. Savary and Guex (199'I, 1999) developed the computer program BioGraph to deal more efficiently with a large volume data, and since that time Hammer, Guex and Savary have developed the UA-Graph program, which is constantly being updated. The UA-Graph version 0.27 (Hammer et aL, 2008) was used in this study.

3.3. Database and procedures

A database was first constructed consisting of selected widely distributed species from biostratigraphic sections in Queen Charlotte Islands, NE British Columbia, Baja California Sur, Japan, Oman, Turkey, Slovenia, Austria, Spain and Argentina.

Each species and subspecies was given a code, which is required by the computer program UA-Graph for range-chart correlation (see Appendix A). Some species codes have been used previously in the calculation of other range charts (Baumgartner et al., 1995; Carter et al., 1998) and these are unchanged. The codes used by Carter et al. (1998) and the new ones designated in the catalogue of Gorican et al. (2006) consist of three letters and two digits; the former represent the genus, the latter species. The codes used by Baumgartner et al. (1995) consist of four digits.

For example, Baumgartner et al. (1995) used all species included in the catalogue whereas in the zonation for the Rhaetian and Hettangian- Sinemurian (Carter, 1993; Carter et al., 1998) and in the Middle Jurassic- Cretaceous zonation of the Budva Zone (Gorican, 1994), all species that occurred in only one sample were eliminated from the zonation as well as some others that were considered too variable to give precise results.
