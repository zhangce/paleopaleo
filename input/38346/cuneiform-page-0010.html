2.1.2. Toarcian, Aatenian and Lower Bajocian

Data for Middle-Late Toarcian, early Aalenian and Early Bajocian faunas are tal<en from Carter et al. (1988) who proposed detailed zonation for this interval based on samples collected from the Whiteaves, Phantom Creek and Graham Island formations mainly from central Graham Island, QCI. The data are much coarser than those provided for the Pliensbachian as fewer samples were available from the heavily wooded interior of the island.

The Whiteaves and Phantom Creek formations are exposed mainly

with irregular limestone beds and concretions. The upper part of the formation is a more resistant unit of much coarser, thicker bedded sandstone with rare calcareous sandstone interbeds. Radiolarian collec- tions from the Fannin Formation are indicated on sections 4 and 8 (Maude Island), section S (Cumshewa Inlet), section 7 (Whiteaves Bay) and section 10 (Rennell Junction). Radiolarian collections from the lower part of the Fannin Formation (Rennell Junction Member) are the richest and best preserved of all Pliensbachian collections; they are associated with ammonites of the Whiteavesi and Freboldi zones, and are late Early Pliensbachian in age (Smith et al., 1988; Smith and Tipper, 1996).
