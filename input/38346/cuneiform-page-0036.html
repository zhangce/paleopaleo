Well-preserved radiolarians from thick contin- uous stratigraphic sections in Queen Charlotte Islands provide the most detailed record for this time interval and form the backbone of this study. These faunas are from measured sections on central Graham Island, Sl<idegate Inlet, Cumshewa Inlet and I<unga Island together with samples from isolated localities on Graham Island, Maude Island and Louise Island, where they are commonly associated with ammonites. Collections from nearly all other areas around the world lack independent dating.

A database of selected widely distributed species was constructed from biostratigraphic sections in all localities mentioned previously and then treated with the Unitary Association (UA) method. An initial sequence of 21 UAs (including ammonite data) was determined for the Pliensbachian and Toarcian from Queen Charlotte Island material only. This included hypothetical assemblages for the top of the Sinemurian (data from Carter et al., 1998) and for the base of the Aalenian (data from Carter et al. '1988; Carter and Jakobs, 1991) in order to extend the ranges of species that cross the range-chart boundaries. Data from other areas were added subsequently produc- ing a final dataset of 197 species and a calculated protoreferential of 41 UAs. The first and the last UAs represent the Late Sinemurian and the Early Bajocian respectively. The remaining 39 UAs were divided into nine zones according to prominent radiolarian faunal breaks and ammonite data. Nine Unitary Association zones (UAZ) are defined: four Early Pliensbachian, one Late Pliensbachian, one Early Toarcian, one Middle-Late Toarcian, and two Aalenian.
