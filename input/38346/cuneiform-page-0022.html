If more than one section was studied in an area by the same author, local range charts were produced and included in the final calculation as one section. This "merging" procedure is required to put equal weight on different areas and not on individual sections (detailed explanation in Guex, 1991). The final dataset thus consists of a set of local range charts (Appendix D). This dataset also includes younger samples up to the Early-Middle Bajocian (data from Yao, 1997) in order to connect the current zonation with that of Baumgartner et al. (1995). To obtain true stratigraphic ranges for younger species, the correlation had to be enhanced by adding two hypothetical levels on top of the QCI range chart, the first for the upper Aalenian and the second for the Lower Bajocian (QCI levels 22 and 23 in Appendix D). These hypothetical levels contain species known to range higher than the initial QCI range chart.
