The holotype and only specimen consists of impressions and fragments of the sacrum, pelvic girdle and both femora and it was originally described as a compsognathid theropod dinosaur but, as Ostrom (1978) notes, it cannot be a theropod. The pterosaurian nature of Herbstosaurus is shown by the presence of a prepubis (Bona- parte, 1978), the long low shape of the ilium (Ostrom, 1978), and the form of the femur in which the shaft curves in the same transverse plane as the head that is offset medially on a prominent neck (Ostrom, 1978). Bonaparte (1978) gives no reasons for the referral of Herbstosaurus to the Pterodactyloidea but it was presumably because there are five sacral ribs. In rhamphorhynchoids the number of sa- cral vertebrae is either three or four whereas in pterodactyloids the number ranges from four to ten {Wellnhofer, 1978).

The only other rhamphorhynchoid de- scribed from the New World is the incomplete skeleton of Xesodactylus hesperius Colbert, 19S9 from the Lower Oxfordian of western Cuba.
