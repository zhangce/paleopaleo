The closest approach to the form of metacarpal IV of Comodactylus in dorsal and ventral views (Text-figs. 2A, C) is that of certain Lower Jurassic and Upper Triassic rhamphorhynchoids. The proximal end of metacarpal IV is relatively wide in the rhamphorhynchoid Campylognathoi des (Early Jurassic; Wellnhofer, 1974), the dimorphodon- tids Peteinosaurus (Late Triassic; Text-fig.

2P) and Dimorphodon (Early Jurassic; YPM), and the eudimorphodontid Eudimorphodon (Wild, 1978). However, the Rhamphorhyn- chidae is the only family of the Rhampho- rhynchoidea recognized to date from the Late Jurassic (Wellnhofer, 1978) so Comodactylus is probably referable to this family. From the measurements given by Wellnhofer (1975b, 1978) and Wild (1975, 1978), it would appear that the holotype of Comodactylus ostromi with a maximum length of 57.5 mm is the larg- est metacarpal IV of a rhamphorhynchoid de- scribed to date.
