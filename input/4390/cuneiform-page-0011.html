Thus two individuals of the Early Jurassic genera Dorygnathus and Camphylo- gnathus with metacarpal IV lengths of 29 mm and 30 mm have forelimb lengths of 506 mm and 862 mm respectively (Wellnhofer, 1978). Consequently any attempt to estimate the total length of the forelimb of Comodactylus would be very approximate. Comodactylus, how- ever, was probably larger than any other rhamphorhynchoid described to date and, judging from the measurements given by Wellnhofer (1970, 1978), it was also larger than any pterodactyloids from the Upper Ju- rassic with the possible exception of "Ptero dactylus" grandis from the Upper Jurassic of Germany, a humerus of which measures 130 mm (Wellnhofer, 1970).
