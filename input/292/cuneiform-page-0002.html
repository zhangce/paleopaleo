Thereafter, the fossils of Aturia coxi were reported from the uppermost Miocene Black Rock For- mation in Beaumaris of Southern Australia, the middle to upper Miocene formations in New Zealand (Beu, 1973), and the up- permost Miocene Senhata Formation in the Boso Peninsula of central Japan (Tomida, 1983).

The present report is the second record of Aturia coxi Miller, 1947 from Japan. The discovery of the nautiloid fossil is im- portant as it can be used to infer marine climate and ocean current at that time; this is because it is a necroplankton as well as a tropical nektobenthos. The systematic description of Aturia coxi provided in this paper is based on the specimen recently found in the uppermost Miocene Otsuki Formation of central Japan.
