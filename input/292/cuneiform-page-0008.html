To the north of the Boso Peninsula, warm tem- perate molluscan faunas occur in the contemporaneous late Miocene deposits in the Sendai area, northern Honshu (38 N). At the same time, a warm planktonic foraminiferal fauna in- vaded from the south, and occurs in the upper Miocene for- mations in the Japan Sea side of Northeast Japan, and also in the Ishikari and Hidaka areas (42.5'N) in southwestern Hok- kaido. The convergence of warm and cold oceanic currents was located in northern Honshu about 3' north of its present position (Ozawa and Tomida, 1992).
