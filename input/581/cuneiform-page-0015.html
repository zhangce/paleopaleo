Cambromedusa differs from Cyclome dusa by possessing radial canals that bifurcate near the umbrellar margin. Other similar gen- era are Ediacaria, Discophyllum, Velumbrel la, Protolyella, and Eldonia. The separation of Cambromedusa and Cyclomedusa from Ediacari a depends largely on interpretation of exumbrellar and internal or subumbrellar fea- tures. Ediacaria and Cyclomedusa may have both concentric rugae and radial strands or grooves; however, in Ediacana the con- centric rugae and the radial grooves are thought to result from compression of the originally domal exumbrella onto a nearly fiat surface (Glaessner & Wade, 1966, p. 603; Wade, 1972, p. 212, text-fig. 7). In Cyclome dusa the concentric rugae and radial strands are thought to be compressional features rep- resenting the exurnbrellar surface and internal structures, respectively (Wade, 1972, p. 204). Tentacles on the subumbrellar surface of a specimen, possibly Cyclomedusa davidi, were illustrated by Wade (1972, p. 204, 206, pl. 41, fig. 2).
