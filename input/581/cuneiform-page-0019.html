Radial canals of C. furcula superficially re- semble the radial grooves of Ediacaria Pin- dersi from the Precambrian, but are of inter- nal rather than of external origin. These species also differ by the lack of concentric rugae in C. furcula. Reconstructions of E. flindersi by Sprigg (1947, text-fig. 3A; 1949, text-figs. 5A,B) show radial canals with abun- dant bifurcations; however, Glaessner R Wade (1966, p. 603) concluded that little branching or overlapping of radial furrows actually exists, contrary to the striking ar- rangement of canals drawn in Sprigg's resto- ration. Alternatively, Glaessner R Wade (1966, p. 603) and Wade (1972, p. 212, text- fig. 4) interpreted the radial grooves on E. flin dersi as surficial subumbrellar features formed by compression and infilling with sand.
