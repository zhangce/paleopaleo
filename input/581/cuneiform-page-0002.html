Elsewhere, Cambrian medusoids are known from Sweden (Torrell, 1870; Walcott, 1898), Poland (Sta- sinska, 1960), Czechoslovakia (Pompeckj, 1896; Walcott, 1898), and the Soviet Union (Milashev, 1958).

Imprints of two types of medusoids have recently been recovered from Middle Cambri- an rocks in Utah. Four specimens of one species have been collected from the Spence Tongue of the Lead Bell Shale, and one spec- imen (part and counterpart) of another species has been collected from the Wheeler Shale. Although few in number, these specimens are of interest because they add to a meager knowledge of fossil medusoids and they pro- vide documentation of the total Cambrian fau- na, which is relatively poorly known except for trilobites and, to a lesser extent, brachio- pods.
