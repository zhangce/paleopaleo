IX. STRUCTURE

The structure map (Fig, 10) shows two major folds (the Knots and Sykes anticlines) with southerly to south-westerly trends, separated by more gently folded country, the whole area being traversed by a regular pattern of north-westerly trending normal faults which mainly throw down to the south-west. There is a lack of continuity of minor structures across certain faults (for example, the Artie Beck, Helks and Smeer Hall faults), which is such as to suggest that at one stage in their history these faults and the folds were forming together.

A few miles to the south-west of the area under discussion the folds disappear under almost horizontal beds of New Red Sandstone, which they therefore predate. Similar features were noted by Selwyn Turner (1935, p. 143), who also considers that most of these folds are posthumous upon underlying Caledonian structures (1949, p. 288}. In the New Red area just referred to, there are also faults with north-westerly trends and south-westerly downthrows similar to those mentioned above, which appear to have moved both before and after the deposition of the New Red Sandstone. The later phase of faulting is probably of Tertiary age.
