In its most southerly outcrops, between the rivers Roeburn and Hind- burn, the Clintsfiield Grit is a fairly coarse felspathic sandstone. This coal is best developed south of High Bentham, where it was worked a good deal during the last century. It is also present in Crossdale (664.651), but is of a much inferior quality, and it extends northwards to the Greta valley.

THE KINDERscoUTIAN STAGE (Ri)

(a) The Bentham Grit Group (Upper)

This stage includes the major part of the Bentham Grit Group and is about 600 feet thick. A most interesting feature is the existence of brachiopod beds, first seen near the base of the stage. These animals presumably migrated from the north, and were able to exist in less favour- able conditions than either the goniatites or the lamellibranchs (probably semi-marine), usually being found in sandy shales. In the upper part of 'the stage there is a considerable development of red, violently false-bedded sandstone, lithologically very difFerent from the underlying strata. This may indicate that there had been a change in the source from which the sediments were derived, and the change may have been related in some way to the incoming of the brachiopods.
