(d) Kinderscoutian (R,)

The isopachytes for this stage show a considerable spread of strata across both the northern and southern massifs. The northern massif and the

northern part of the basin are characterized by sandstones, shales and marine bands. The Cayton Gill Beds, marine sandstones and shales with a brachiopod fauna, are near the base of the stage and extend as far south as Keighley. Similar brachiopod beds occur in the Bentham Grit Group, although in this case the vertical range is greater. Apart from the brachiopod beds, the Bentham sequence is more attenuated than else- where and is very different lithologically, In the southern part of the Central Pennine Basin the great thicknesses of the Kinderscout Grits are equivalent to the Bentham Grits.

(e) Marsdenian {Rs)

That the basin exercised less control on sedimentation during this stage is indicated by the absence of sudden thinning near the margins of the massifs. Lithogically the Bentham sequence is rather different from those elsewhere but can be correlated with them by means of marine bands. The R. gractle marine band at the base of the stage is a widespread horizon throughout the north of England, and the Greta Grit Group which follows is undoubtedly the same age as the Middle Grits of the other parts of the Pennines. The Middle Grits usually dominate the Namurian both in thickness and in the number of marjne horizons they contain. In con- trast, the Greta Grits are insignificant in the Lancaster Fells sequence.
