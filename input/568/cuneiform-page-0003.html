(a) The Upper Bowland Shales

The Upper Bowland Shales, together with the underlying Lower Bow- land Shalea and Visban limeetonea, are exposed only in the south-east between the Sykes and Whitendale valleys. They are also well exposed in the adjacent Slaidburn district, where the sequence which wae described by Parkinson in 1936 is similar to that mentioned herein. The lowest beds exposed in the Lancaster Fella area are to be seen where the Sykea anti- cline crosses the Sykes, Brennand and Whitendale valleys and consist of Visban limestones which appear to be overlain unconformably and with overlap by Lower Bowland Shales, also of Visban age. The present work, however, ie concerned only with the Namurian, and much more detailed mapping will be necessary before any definite conclusions can be drawn concerning the Lower Carboniferous.

The Lower Bowland Shales {Lower Carboniferous) and the Upper Bowland Shalea (Upper Carboniferous) are conformable, the dividing line between them being a faunal one in the middle of incompletely exposed shale and therefore a very dificult line to map. There are no good sections across the junction and it has not been possible to obtain a detailed faunal succession from the Lower to the Upper Carboniferous.
