{ii) The North-Easterly Trending Faults

These faults are small and for the most part are normal faults with north-westerly downthrows.

The Millers House fault in the south is quite important and coincides with the northern margin of the Sykes anticline, to which it is probably related. Farther north, other examples of these faults occur on Grit Pell and Clougha (545.595) and have north-westerly downthrows of the order of 50 feet. In the extreme north, in Claughton Quarries (564.660), a reversed fault with a maximum downthrow of 160 feet to the north-west runs along the axis of the Quernmore syncline until it is cut oK by the north-westerly trending Claughton fault.
