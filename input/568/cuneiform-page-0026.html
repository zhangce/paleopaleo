Dr. B. G. S. HUnsox congratulated Dr. Moseley on the courage and thoroughness with which he had successfully mapped such an extensive area of fell country. He was glad that the author had acknowledged that his results were possible only because of the pioneer work of Tiddeman, and Dr. Hudson recognized with pleasure that, notwithstanding Tidde- man's lack of knowledge of the goniatite succession, his map did not differ appreciably from that of the author. The speaker appreciated the work of the author in constructing a clear and valuable stratigraphical succes- sion of the Namurian of the Lancaster Fells but was rather perturbed at the clear-cut simplicity of his faunal lists and the ease of his faunal correlation with other areas. The author had, for instance, compared his succession with those of Simonseat and Rombald Moor. Had he examined the faunas from these areas > If not, he was making correlations based on comparable sedimentary successions and not on faunal succes- sions. He (the speaker) hoped the author had made that clear in his paper and that he would later publish a palaeontological account of his faunas.
