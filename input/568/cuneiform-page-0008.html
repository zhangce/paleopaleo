Icor stratigraphical purposes Cravenoceras and Cravenoceratoidea are the most important genera. They occur in bands of very fine-grained black shale, never more than a few inches thick, from which the other abundant goniatite genus, Anthracoceraa, is almost completely absent. Usually there are about four of these bands in the Ct. linfer beds, the lowest one often resting directly on the undulating surface of the Roeburndale Grit. The scarcity of Anthracoceras in the bands ia shown by the following percentages: Cravenoceraa and Cravenoceratoides 37, Anthracoceras (with Dimorphoceras) 3, Poeidonia and Poaidoniella 54, Orthoceras etc. 4 {average of 1235 fossils). The shales between the Cravenoeeratoulea bands are quite fossiliferous, but in this ease Anthracoceraa is the dominant goniatite and Cravenocerae and Cravenoceratoides are completely absent. Above the Ct. lirifer beds, between 12 and 25 feet above the Roeburndale Grit, the shale becomes a little micaceous, It contains few fossils but occasional specimens of Anthracoceras, lamellibrancha and Orthoceraa are present.
