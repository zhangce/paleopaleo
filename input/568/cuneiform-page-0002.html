THE area described in this paper lies almost entirely in North Lancashire. Most of it consists of peat-covered. Millstone Grit moorlands, which rise to over 1800 feet on Littledale Fell and slope gently to the low-lying Ingleton Coalfield in the north and to Lonsdale and Morecambe Bay in the north-west and west. In the south-east the high ground descends more abruptly to the Lower Carboniferous anticlinal areas of the Brennand, Whitendale and Hodder valleys.

The exposure is usually good, especially on the dissected mar'gins of the fells, where most streams yield continuous sections and escarpments and dip-slopes are common.
