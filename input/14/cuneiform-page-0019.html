This specimen from Hulusitai, Xingxia, China is conspecific with Turbonitella semisulcatus (Phillips) from the Lower Carboniferous "Bolland" of Northern England in shape, number and position of tubercles extending to midwhorl, but divers from it in having a slightly smaller shell. The species also resembles Turbonitella biserialis (Phillips) from the "Bolland" in shape, but divers in that the latter bears two rows of tubercles, one confined to the shoulder, the second to the outer portion of the base. The lower tubercle is very weak and may in some cases disappear. If the upper tubercles are slightly extended, it is very difficult to seperate the two species, so the two species may be closely related.
