These well-preserved specimens weather out from siltstone and mud-limestone, so their ornamentation is clearly seen. This assemblage is dominated by more than 50 specimens of Eu- phemites, including three species: Euphemites hindi (Weir), E. urei (Fleming), and E. konincki (Weir). Secondarily important elements are Turbonitella semisulcatus (Phillips) and Naticopsis. Turbonitella semisulcatus is represented by a single well-pre- served specimen; this is the first occurrence of the genus in China. Six specimens are referred to two species of Naticopsis: Naticopsis (N) variata (Phillips) and N. (N.?) ovoidea de Kon- inck. Murchisonia (Donaldospira) gradussiformis (Licharev) is known from two specimens, and Strobeus rectilinea (Phillips) from a single well-preserved specimen. These species are sig- nificant for biostratigraphic correlation and paleobiogeographic interpretation.
