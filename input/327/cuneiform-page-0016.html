It also suggests that if Amplexizaphrentis, which is one of the more common and widespread corals in the Mississippian of the western United States (Sando and Bamber, 1985), is to be of biostratigraphic value, species must be described from large samples taking into account intraspecific variability. Although no consistent subgroupings of specimens could be differentiated in this col- lection on the basis of multiple morphologic characters, it is possible that two or more species that exhibit considerably over- lapping ranges of morphologic variability could be present. If this is the case, the present sample (47 specimens) is insufficient to recognize and differentiate such variable and overlapping species.
