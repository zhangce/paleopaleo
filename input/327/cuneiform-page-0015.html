Ontogenetic trends in variability are evident in Table 1 and include shortening of the cardinal septum, shift from pinnate to radial septal pattern, decline of alar fossulae, and lengthening of minor septa through ontogeny. Presumed ecophenotypic variability is demonstrated by the distribution of specimens with excessive amounts of stereoplasm. The majority of mature specimens exhibiting mas- sive amounts of stereoplasm occur at a single locality (loc. 2A). Mature specimens from loc. 1 mostly exhibit little stereoplasmic lamination. Specimens from all populations appear to be iden- tical in other respects and there does not appear to be a large stratigraphic separation between the populations. The occur- rence of excessive amounts of stereoplasm thus appears to cor- relate to specific localities or environments of deposition, and thus may be related to unknown, local environmental eftects. The remaining variability, including cardinal fossula shape, sep- tal pattern, length or major septa, and septal ratio, is considered to represent normal intraspecific variability for this species. This level of variability is disturbing because these features, especially fossula shape, amount of bilateral symmetry, and degree of with- drawal of major septa, commonly have been used to define species in zaphrentoid corals.
