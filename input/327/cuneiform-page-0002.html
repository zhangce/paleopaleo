The Imo Formation is correlative with Coral Zone VI of the Western Interior Province of North America (Sando and Bam- ber, 1985). Zone VI is the least well known of Sando and Bam- ber's (1985) Mississippian coral zones and is characterized by only six coral genera. Of these, only the long-ranging Amplex- izaphrentis and possibly the Zone V holdover, Barytichisma, occur in the Imo Formation. Other Imo corals include I.opho- phyllidium, which elsewhere occurs only in the Pennsylvanian and Permian, Bradyphyllum, which is characteristic of"middle" Carboniferous (typically Lower Pennsylvanian) strata, and the endemic tabulate, Tectamichelinia, which occurs in overlying Morrowan strata. The Imo coral fauna thus contains elements that are characteristic of both the Mississippian and Pennsyl- vanian. A similar relationship was described by Burdick and Strimple (1973) for Imo crinoid species and by Hoare et al. (1989) for Imo bivalves. Webb (1987) stated that the coral fauna of the underlying Pitkin Formation likewise exhibits both Mis- sissippian and Pennsylvanian a%nities, despite the occurrence of only two genera, and no species, common to the Imo and Pitkin Formations.
