Variability. â€” The internal morphology of this species is ex- tremely variable; the most variable characteristics are sum- marized in Table 1. The length of the cardinal fossula in trans- verse section varies considerably, but is approximately one-half, or more, of the coral diameter in a given thin section. The shape of the cardinal fossula varies between parallel-sided (Figure 5.16), axially expanded (Figure 5.11), axially narrowing (Figure 5.14), and keyhole-shaped (Figure 5.21). Most juveniles exhibit par- allel-sided (Figure 5.2, 5.12) or axially expanding fossulae (Fig- ure 5.23), whereas mature specimens exhibit a more even dis- tribution of fossula shapes with axially expanding types most prevalent. The cardinal septum shortens through ontogeny (Fig- ure 5.2, 5.4, 5.12, 5.14, 5.15, 5.16). The septal pattern is mostly pinnate in juvenile sections, but becomes very evenly distrib- uted between pinnate and radial in mature sections. This shift towards radial symmetry is accompanied by a decline in the development of the alar fossulae. The occurrence of withdrawn major septa also increases through ontogeny although nearly equal proportions of sections with withdrawn and non-with- drawn septa exist in mature specimens. The septal ratio is also quite variable throughout ontogeny. Minor septa show a general trend to lengthen through ontogeny, but rarely project through the stereoplasm of the wall. The abundance of stereoplasm is highly variable at all ontogenetic stages.
