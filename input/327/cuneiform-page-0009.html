Discussion. â€” Bradyphyllum lesliense n. sp. exhibits much on- togenetic and intraspecific variability. Ontogenetic variability reflects the transition from a zaphrentoid juvenile stage to an amplexoid adult stage. Intraspecific variability is expressed in the length of the zaphrentoid stage, the length and thickness of the major septa, the degree of lateral fusion of the septa on the tops of tabulae, and the relative amount of stereoplasmic lam- ination. Bradyphyllum lesliense is very similar in size and ap- pearance to B. clavigerum, from the Upper Mississippian or Lour Pennsylvanian Cameron Creek Formation of central Montana (Easton, 1962), but is distinguished by more promi- nent fusion of the septa on the tops of tabulae. Tabulae also appear to be more numerous in B. clavigerum.
