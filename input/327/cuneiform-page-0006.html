Discussion. â€” Bradyphyllum was originally described by Gra- bau (1928) as a streptelasmoid coral with a stereocolumn, thick, radially arranged major septa, accelerated insertion in counter quadrants, and short cardinal and counter septa. Because of the rather vague description and poor illustrations of the type spe- cies, B. bellicostatum, the character of the genus was not well defined. Fomichev (1953) emended the generic diagnosis noting that, although the ends of septa are united axially in juvenile stages, septa are withdrawn with free ends in later stages. He also noted that Grabau (1928) had mistakenly used the term "dissepiments" for tabulae and that numerous complete and incomplete tabulae occur in the type species. He further noted that the counter septum is only rarely shortened and that the cardinal septum is not shortened in all sections. Fomichev (1953) erected three new species, and a possible fourth, from the Donetz Basin. Easton (1962) further clarified this genus by stating that major septa are amplexoid and expand axially on the tops of tabulae. He stressed that a stereocolumn is not present in adult growth stages. He also stated that there is no continuous inner wall around the aulos as in Amplexocarinia.

Bradyphyllum appears to be most closely related to Roti- phyllum Hudson, but is distinguished on the basis of slightly withdrawn, amplexoid major septa in mature stages.
