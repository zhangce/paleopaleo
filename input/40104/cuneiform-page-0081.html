Polusocrinus rosa appears to be closely related to P. ochelataensis, from which it may have been derived. P. rosa has much more robust plates, giving longer but similar ar- ticular facets, and the infrabasal circlet is taller, such that the IBB plates are readily visible in side view. P. ochelataensis has much thinner plates. Pabian and Strimple (1980a, p. 16) suggested that P. rosa first appeared in the Virgilian, and the examples at hand support this observation.
