Northrop (1939, p. 153) stated that the occurrence of Cysti- halysites amplitubulatus was "probably Bouleaux Formation" (=Bouleaux Facies of the West Point Formation, Bourque and Lachambre, 1980). Since thorough collection of Tabulata from the Bouleaux Facies in the course of the present study has not turned up any specimens of this species, it is more likely that Lambe's specimens come from the Gascons Formation and that this species is restricted to the latter.

C. ampliIubulata described and il- lustrated by Stumm (1962) from northern Maine is consistent with C. ampIEtubulatus in major characters and is probably conspecific with it. Stumm's specimens are of late Early Silurian or early Late Silurian age, and are thus slightly older than the Gaspe material.
