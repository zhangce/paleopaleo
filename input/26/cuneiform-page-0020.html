Syringopora vestita Tchudinova (1971) from the Silurian of Podolia, also discussed and illustrated by Stasinska (1974) from the Silurian of northeastern Poland, possesses thin-walled, broadly spaced corallites with diameters (outside) of 0.4-0.6 mm. It divers from the specimens described here as S.? sp. cf. S. vestita by apparently having septa that are greatly reduced or absent. Syringopora. sp. cf. S. vestita is the only species of this genus from the Silurian of the Baie des Chaleurs region occurring in stromatoporoid coenostea. It is found in some of the same facies and sections as two other small-corallite species, S. com- pacta Billings and S. minuta n. sp. Syringopora. sp. cf. S. vestita is distinguished from both of these by its significantly smaller tabularium diameter and rare transverse stolons, as well as its growth habit.
