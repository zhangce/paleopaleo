The Gascons and West Point Formations were deposited over a considerable period of time from the late Early to mid-Late Silurian (middle Wenlock to latest Ludlow-equivalent). Most sections are located in the Port-Daniel and Black Cape areas (Figure 1), and exposure of both formations is very good. Strati- graphic ranges of the halysitid and auloporid species (Figure 2) are extremely varied, and are related in some degree to facies patterns. In particular, the extreme rarity of tabulate corals in large portions of the shales and siltstones of the Gascons For- mation and in crinoid shoals of the Anse McInnis Facies of the West Point Formation may have strongly affected the apparent stratigraphic ranges of several species. This, combined with the high degree of endemism of the auloporid and halysitid faunas, would make stratigraphic comparison with other regions ex- tremely difficult.

The facies distribution of each species was determined by combining data on orientation, breakage, colony growth form, and growth relationships of several groups of tabulate corals with data on associated faunas and lithologies and with infor- mation from other paleoecologic studies. The latter include the work of Pickerill et al. (1977), Bourque (1979, 1984), Bourque and Lachambre (1980), and Bourque et al. (1986).

The general distribution of tabulate corals in the various facies of the West Point Formation has been extensively described by Lee and Noble (1988b), and readers are referred to that publi- cation for further information, but very little has been written about coral occurrence in the Gascons Formation.
