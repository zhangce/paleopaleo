Halysitids and auloporids from the Baie des Chaleurs region have been discussed and illustrated in the important early work of Lambe (1899) and in later studies by Northrop (1939), Wright and Bourque (1980), and Bolton (1981b). Gaspe specimens were included by Young and Noble (1987a, 1987b) in studies of Early Silurian syringoporids and halysitids from New Brunswick. Twenhofel (1928) and Bolton (19& la) have studied members of these taxa from Anticosti Island, and Shrock and Twenhofel (1939) described and illustrated some halysitids from northern Newfoundland.

This study is intended to complement other recent work that we have carried out on the coral faunas of the Gascons and West Point Formations. This includes descriptions of West Point Formation heliolitids (Lee and Noble, 198&a), a discussion of West Point coral paleoecology (Lee and Noble, 1988b), work on Baie des Chaleurs Heliolitida including Gascons Formation species (Young and Noble, 1990a, 1990b), a study of variation in the syringoporid symbiont Syringopora. sp. cf. S. vestita (Young and Noble, 1989), and a review of tabulate coral bio- stratigraphy and biofacies of this region (Young and Noble, 1990c). In addition to this regional aspect, another major ob- jective of this study is to describe variation in these taxa as part of longer term work on general trends in tabulate coral vari- ability.

The halysitids and auloporids of the Gascons and West Point Formations show a high degree of endemism. Among the hal- ysitids, Cystihalysites amplitubulatus (Lambe) may also occur only in northern Maine. Halysites nitidus Lambe may possibly occur in British Columbia, Kentucky, and Ohio. Of the aulo- porids, Syringopora minuta n. sp. and Pseudofletcheria? sp. 1 are both restricted to the southern Gaspe, and Syringopora'? sp. cf. S. vestita Tchudinova may be conspecific with a species found in Poland and in Podolia, U.S.S.R. By comparison, the older Limestone Point and La Vieille Formations of northern New Brunswick and the Anse a Pierre-Loiselle and La Vieille For- mations of the southern Gaspe have 10 species of halysitids, of which three are endemic, and five species of auloporids, of which two are endemic.
