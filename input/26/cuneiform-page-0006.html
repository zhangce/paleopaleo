Tabulate corals are abundant in the pebble and cobble con- glomerates and breccias that occur in approximately the same position near the top of each of the three major Gascons For- mation sections studied. Clasts in these beds are about 10-300 mm in diameter, composed largely of skeletal material, and well- rounded to angular (Figure 3.2, 3.3). Coral colonies may be relatively complete and attached to sediment within a clast, or they may be fragmented. The beds have a matrix of fine-grained silt and mud. Bourque et al. (1986) considered these beds to be fore-reef debris deposits, representative of intermediate water depths below wave base. The diversity and abundance of corals and stromatoporoids, the common occurrence of overgrowths within clasts, and the presence of sediment attached to corals in single clasts suggesting early lithilcation prior to clast trans- port (Mcllreath and James, 1984) are consistent with reefal origins. However, these beds are quite variable in character and more than one type of transportation may have occurred.
