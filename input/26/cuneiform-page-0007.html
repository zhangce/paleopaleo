Auloporids in these formations are also generally rare, but are locally common or abundant in some facies. The free-living Syringopora minuta is abundant in the allochthonous beds at the top of the Gascons Formation, but absent elsewhere in that unit, and also occurs in small numbers in the shaley limestones of the Bouleaux Facies of the West Point Formation. By con- trast, Syringopora. sp. cf. S. vestita, which occurs inside stro- matoporoid skeletons (the "caunopore" life mode), is much more common in these formations. It is found in both the au- tochthonous and allochthonous assemblages in the Gascons Formation, and in several open-platform and patch-reef facies of the West Point Formation.
