PROCEDURES

Material was collected from 10 measured sections in the New Richmond and Port-Daniel areas of the southern Gaspe Pen- insula (Figure 1). All colonies were slabbed and polished, and acetate peels and a number of thin sections were made.

Terms used to describe internal morphology are in common usage and are derived from Buehler (1955), Laub (1979), and Hill (1981) with slight modification. Some of Young and Scrut- ton's (in press) terms are used to describe external colony form. Species are described by both qualitative and quantitative methods, since tabulate corals possess several characters that are easily measured and give a good representation of variation, but such data must be carefully interpreted.

Quantitatively analyzed features were, in part, measured using the techniques defined by Dixon (1974, 1979) for the Helioliti- dae. Twenty measurements of each character were taken where possible on each section to provide data representative of the colony (Dixon, 1974; C. T. Scrutton, personal commun.). Fea- tures were measured to the nearest 0.05 mm. Numerically de- fined characters are described below.
