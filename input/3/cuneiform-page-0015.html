New sites discovered in the last two years yielded well-preserved sin- gle valves and shell interiors, thus making possible a detailed taxonomic study and comparison with the Siberian material (Fig. I). In the Anticosti sequence, Kulumbella has a very short strati- graphic range (within about 2 m of calcareous mudstone, co- quinite, calcarenite, or grainstone), and the Kulumbella interval appears to coincide almost with the first occurrence of Penta- merus. In morphological distinctness and geographical prove- nance, the occurrence of ECulumbella parallels that of the Anti- costi rugose coral Palaearaea, previously also known only from Siberia.
