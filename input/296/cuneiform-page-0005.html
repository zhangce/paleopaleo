A few Teredo tubes indicate the presence of some driftwood, which might have been the at- tachment site for the barnacles and rare specimens of Ostrea crenu!i margi nata (Gabb).

Both sites suggest deposition below wave base in shelfal waters greater than 45 m depth. The abundance of detrital feeders and the relative lack of suspension feeders (except those presumably associated with driftwood) indicate a high-turbidity environ- ment. Based on grain-size and the relative abundance of Teredo masses, the Butler County site appears to have been located closer to shore.
