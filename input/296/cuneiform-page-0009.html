Most of the carinae of A. toulmini appear to be from juveniles and, except for the absence of ridges bordering the tectum, are essentially identical in intraparietal and tectal development to juvenile carinae of A. conradi. However, the few basal fragments representing larger, presumably more mature individuals lack the basal continua- tion of the intraparietes developed in adult carinae of A. conradi. The structure of the carina and the more conservative shape of the tergum and carinal latus are transitional between Cretaceous species of the A. fossula group and A. conradi, and suggest that the geologically older A. tou1mini is ancestral to A. conradi.
