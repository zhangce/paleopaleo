The capitular and peduncular plates of A. conradi have been described, as well as the derivation of A. conradi from the Upper Cretaceous A. fossula Darwin group (Zullo, 1990). How- ever, the relationship of A. conradi to the older Paleocene species A. toulmini remains obscure because of limited knowledge of the capitulum of the latter species.

Re-collection of the type locality of A. toulmini provided only three partial carinae, but a new locality in the same unit in Wilcox County (Figure 1) has yielded carinae, upper, carinal, inframedian, and rostral latera, peduncle plates, and additional scuta and terga. The Wilcox County scuta and terga are about half the size of those from the type locality and, together with the majority of the other capitular plates, may have come from juveniles. Fortunately, both juvenile and adult capitular plates are known for A. conradi, and changes in plate morphology resulting from allometric growth can be taken into consideration in comparing the two species. Although allometry was dem- onstrated in the development of the carina and upper latus of A. conradi (Zullo, 1990), no significant allometry was observed in the growth of the remaining capitular plates. Comparison of the two species indicates that they are closely related and sug- gests that A. toulmini is also a derivative of the A. fossula group and ancestral to A. conradi.

The Wilcox County locality also yielded worn scuta and a partial tergum that appear to represent the scalpelloid genus Zeugmatolepas Withers. Zeugrnatojepas is predominantly a Mesozoic genus first appearing in the Jurassic, but one European Upper Cretaceous species, Z. cretae (Steenstrup), is known to range into the Danian in Denmark. Although the Porters Creek species cannot be determined based on available material, the scuta bear remarkable resemblance to those of Z. cretae.

PALEOECOLOGY

The sediment at the type locality for Arcosca/pellum toulmini in Butler County is a very fine-grained, slightly glauconitic, quartz sand. Leaching has removed any aragonitic shells. Benthic for- aminifers, especially species of Xodosaria, Dentalina, and I.en- Iiculi na, are abundant and planktonic species are common. The ostracode fauna is sparse and of low diversity, being dominated by species of Bairdia and Cytherella. The cnidarians are rep- resented by rare examples of the gorgonian Graphularia.
