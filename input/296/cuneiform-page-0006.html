Discussion.â€” The first scutum discovered from the Porters Creek Formation was referred to the lepadoid genus Oxynaspis Darwin. However, William A. Newman (personal commun.) noted several characteristics of this scutum that seemed to pre- clude its inclusion in the Lepadoidea, and suggested the possi- bility of its referral to the scalpelloid genus Zeugmatolepas. Further collecting yielded four additional scuta and a partial tergum that support his conclusion.

Withers (1935, p. 92) discussed the considerable variation in the shape of Z. cretae scuta, but because of the lack of sufficient comparative material throughout the Upper Cretaceous, he chose to refer all of the material from the Turonian through the Danian to a single species. Although Withers (193S) did not illustrate scuta from the Danian of Denmark, he did note their similarity to those from the English Mcraster coranguinum Zone spanning the Coniacian-Santonian boundary (Rawson et al., 1978). The Porters Creek scuta also are remarkably similar in form and ornament to M. coranguinum Zone scuta illustrated by Withers (193S, Pl. 3, 6gs. 12, 13) and, thus, could be referred to this European species without hesitation, particularly considering the broad definition of Z. cretae. However, because of the worn condition of the scuta and the virtual absence of other types of capitular plates, the Porters Creek specimens cannot be posi- tively identified with Z. cretae.
