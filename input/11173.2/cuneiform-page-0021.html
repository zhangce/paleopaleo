FIELD STATIONS OF THE TRENT MARL

The fossils collected from the Trent marl are listed in the table on page 14. Notes concerning the. field stations are given below. (See fig. 1.)

10854. In Jacksonville, Onslow County, at east end of wagon bridge over New River. The eastern abutment of the bridge is an outcrop of limestone. Large masses of the rock excavated from the road are piled by the roadside. The exposure is a hard gray impure limestone made up eutirely of casts of bivalves from which most of the shelly material has been dissolved.

10855. Half a mile southeast of Silverdale, Onslow County, on farm of John Gillette, on left side of Webbs Creek. Yellow rotten-shell marl is exposed over a small area in pits about 6 feet deep. The shells are white and lie in a matrix of yellow earthy sand. For the most part the shells are weathered and broken, but many individuals are in a state of perfect preser- vation.
