Parallelism has often been invoked to explain similarities between groups of ro- dents. However, when details of similarity far outweigh differences, the hypothesis of

parallelism must be discarded and the hypothesis of a close phylogenetic relationship

must be adopted. This is especially true when intermediate morphological stages are

recorded in a nearly continuous biochronologic sequence.
