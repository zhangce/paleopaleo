Evidently, small and medium-sized freshwater fishes and tetrapods were unable to colonise the Cornet island, and the same was true for sauropod dinosaurs, lizards and mammals. Theropods and ankylosaurs were also much rarer than might have been expected.

Finally, the dinosaurs from Cornet appear to be dwarfs, and this is another typical feature of islands ( Williamson, 1981).
