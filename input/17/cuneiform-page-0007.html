In Mackenzie Mountains specimens, this boss almost always reaches poste- riorly past the posterior edge of the preocular border furrow (i. e., past the anterior extent of the fixigena; e.g., Siveter and Chatterton, 1996, plate 1, figures 1, 2, 10, 13, 16, 18, 25). In no Arctic specimens does the boss reach the anterior part of the fixigena. This is not a function of differing orientations for pho- tography, as the same situation can be observed when comparing oblique views (cf. Figure 5. 10 and Siveter and Chatterton, 1996, plate 1, figure 17). Nevertheless, in all other respects, the samples seem identical. They are therefore regarded as conspecific [see Adrain and Edgecombe, in press, for a comparable situation with respect to samples of the encrinurine trilobite Struszia dimitrovi (Perry and Chatterton, 1979)].
