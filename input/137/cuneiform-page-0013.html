In order to make explicit the manner in which the names are applied herein, it is necessary to review the somewhat confusing history of clas- sificatio.

Owens and Thomas (1975) described the new genus Aadnoria and placed it within the Brachymetopinae, at that point as the only pre-Devonian member. They further included within Bra- chymetopidae the Warburgellinae Owns, 1973, a taxon gen- erally considered to belong within Proetidae Salter, 1864. The concept of a brachymetopine-warburgelline relationship was criticized at length by Campbell (1977; see also Holloway, 1980), defended by Thomas (1978; also Owens, 1986), but ultimately retracted by Owens (in Owens and Hamman, 1990, p. 239).

Campbell (1977} proposed a subdivision within generally ac- cepted brachymetopids between a nominate subfamily includ- ing Brachymetopus McCoy, 1847, Australosutura Campbell and Goldring in Amos, Campbell, and Goldring, 1960, and Proetides %alter, 1924, and his new Cordaniinae, to which Cordania Clarke, 1892, and Radnoria Owens and Thomas, 1975, were assigned. Cordaniinae, however, was conceived as an expressly paraphyletic taxon, as Campbell (1977, p. 10-11) considered that the genus Mystrocephala Whittington, 1960, "is an Early Devonian contemporary of Cordania and probably represents the divergent morphology from a Aadnoria-like Silurian cor- daniine to the Late Devonian-Carboniferous brachymeto- pines."
