LGCAI.ITIES, STRATIGRAPHY, AND DISTRIBUTION OF TRILOBITES

The stratigraphy of the Siluro-Devonian Hunton Group was documented by Amsden (1960). Campbell (1967) described the Upper Silurian (Ludlow) trilobites of the Henryhouse Forma- tion. Ormiston (1968) dealt with Devonian trilobites from the Turkey Creek Inlier, a unit historically referred to or correlated with the Hunton Group, but shown by Amsden et al. (1968) and Ormiston (1968) to be of probable Emsian age. Campbell (1977) described the Lochkovian trilobites of the Haragan and Bois d Arc Formations, and Pragian forms of the overlying Fris- co Formation (see Amsden et al., 1968, Boucot and Johnson, 1967„and Lundin, 1968„ for discussion of the age and corre- lation of the units).

Campbell's (1977) study was based largely on institutional collections, often with imprecise stratigraphic control. The Har- agan/Bois d'Arc trilobite faunas were essentially treated as an undivided whole, in the absence of comprehensive information on stratigraphic occurrence. In this work, we have gathered information on trilobite occurrences from four separate locali- ties in the area of the Old Hunton Townsite (Figure 1). These localities are scattered around Amsden s (1960) section C 1 (Fig- ure 1.2), a major source of material for Campbell's (1977) work, and represent sites of detailed excavation and sampling by com- mercial and amateur collectors. The localities are as follows:

1) F. Wessman quarry (Figure 1.1). Type locality of Cordania wessmani new species. Positioned immediately north of Ams- den's (1960) line of section C 1, spanning the gradational contact of the upper Haragan and lower Bois d'Arc Formations. This contact is drawn at the first occurrence of nodular and lensoid, tripolitic chert within the sequence of calcilutites and marlstones [disregarding Amsden's thin unit L; see Amsden (1960, p. 185- 186) and below]. The holotype of C. wessmani was collected above this contact, and hence from Amsden's unit P, the Cravatt Member of the Bois d'Arc Formation.

2) R. Carroll Upper Quarry (Figure 1.3). Of similar strati- graphic position to the Wessman quarry, spanning the Haragan/ Bois d'Arc contact.
