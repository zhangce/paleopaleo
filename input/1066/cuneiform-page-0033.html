According to Repen- ning (1983), Enhydriodon? reevei seems to represent the same stage of evolution and per- haps the same species as the mandible from the Gubik Formation, although both taxa are too poorly known to adequately assess their systematic relationships. Considering the age, location, and possible affinities of the Alas- kan specimen, Repenning suggested that En- hydriodon~ reevei may have dispersed from the North Atlantic through the Arctic Ocean into the North Pacific and evolved into En- hydra.

A second phase of this proposed route for late Miocene dispersal from the Pacific to the Atlantic involves the Central American Sea- way. Repenning, Ray and Grigorescu (1979) proposed that odobenine odobenids entered the Caribbean Sea from the Pacific Ocean by way of the then-open Central American Sea- way between 6 and 8 Ma. Enhydritherium terraenovae could have entered the Carib- bean Sea and hence the North Atlantic during this same time period, although like the odobenids, it lacks a fossil record of this age from the Caribbean Basin.
