Better preserved, more complete material from Florida described herein indicates that the North American fossil sea otter repre- sents a distinct new genus. To this new genus we also refer a late Miocene species from Eu- rope, formerly placed in Enhydriodon, E. lluecai from near Tereul, Spain (Villalta and Crusafont-Pairo, 1945; Crusafont-Pairo and Golpe, 1962).

In this paper we describe the Florida sam- ple of this new sea otter and review its North American record. We also consider its sys- tematic amenities, stratigraphic and geograph- ic distribution, and paleoecology.
