However, the two large mustelids know~ from the Upper Bone Valley fauna, Enhydrithe- rium and the wolverine Plesiogulo, would have had tibiae of the same general size and proportions as UF 40087. Hendey (1978) and Harrison (1981) both noted that postcrania of Plesiogulo, are similar to those of Gulo, although generally larger and more robust. Since only one tibia of P/esiogulo has been recorded (Hendey, 1978), and that specimen was not figured or described in detail, the Bone Valley tibia was compared to that of Gulo. The cnemial crest is broader antero- posteriorly in Gulo than in the fossil, but does not extend as far distally. The posterior sur- face of the proximal end in the fossil is deeply pocketed beneath the articular facets and both the lateral and medial edges of this depression are defined by prominent crests. In Gulo, the proximal concavity and lateral crest are lack- ing and the medial crest is weak. The distal half of the shaft in Gulo is round in cross section, while in the fossil the distal extension of the cnemial crest gives the shaft a trian- gular cross section.
