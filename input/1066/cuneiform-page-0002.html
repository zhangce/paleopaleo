The Asian record includes the genotype, E. si- valensis (Falconer, 1868) from the Pliocene, Upper Siwaliks and E. falconeri (Pilgrim, 1931) from the late Miocene, Dhok Pathan Formation, Siwalik Hills, India. In South Af- rica, E. africanus has been reported from the Pliocene at Kleinzee (Stromer, 1931) and at Langebaanweg (Hendey, 1978). Howell and Petter (1979) referred an unnamed very large new species to this genus from the middle Pliocene, Hadar Formation, East Africa. We agree with Repenning (1976} and query the taxonomic assignment of Enhydriodon'? la- tipes (Pilgrim, 1931) from the late Miocene of Pikermi, Greece and Enhydriodon'? reevei (Newton, 1890) from the late Pliocene, Nor- wich Crag of England. Both species are poorly known. E.'? reevei appears to be more closely related to the living sea otter, Enhydra (Re- penning, 1983).
