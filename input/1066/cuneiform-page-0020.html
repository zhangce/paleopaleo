A fragment of the upper limb, UF 67973, distal end of right humerus is provisionally referred to Enhydritherium terraenovae. Measurements (mm) of UF 67973 are: trans- verse diameter, 32.0 and anteroposterior di- ameter, 17.5. As was true of the tibia, the most favorable comparisons of the fossil were made with Gulo and Enhydra. In the fossil as in Enhydra the supracondylar ridge is in- conspicuous in degree of development and flattened. In Gulo, this ridge is prominent and it projects strongly posterolaterally. The fos- sil diflers from Enhydra and is most similar to Gulo in the shape of the medial epicondyle. In both Enhydritherium and Gulo the lateral margin of the epicondyle is triangular and flattened anteroposteriorly rather than rect- angular and deep as in Enhydra.
