BIOCHRONOLOGY

Enhydritherium terraenovae is presently known from ten early to late Hemphillian faunas in California and Florida, some of which are almost exclusively marine. For- tunately, all of these occurrences are in as- sociation with at least a few terrestrial mam- mals allowing for biostratigraphic correlation and placement within the North American Land Mammal biochronology.

Barnes et al. (1981) described an isolated M, (LACM 121591) which they referred to Enhydriodon aff E. lluecai from the upper gravel conglomerate unit of the San Mateo Formation at Oceanside, California.
