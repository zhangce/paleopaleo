The simplest explanation of the occurrence of the closely related Enhydritherium species on opposite sides of the North Atlantic in the late Miocene is dispersal directly around the northern rim of the Atlantic Ocean from Eu- rope to the Gulf of Mexico. A North Atlantic distribution of the same or closely related species of phocids in the early Pliocene has been documented (Ray, 1976; Repenning, Ray and Grigorescu, 1979). This hypothesis would explain the earliest North American occurrence of Enhydritherium in the Gulf of Mexico and it could be coupled with pro- posed dispersal through the Central Ameri- can Seaway in the late Miocene, if the direc- tion is reversed, from the Caribbean to the North Pacific.
