Florida occurrences represent more diverse depositional environments and therefore sample a broader variety of potential habi- tats. The upper part of the Bone Valley For- mation appears to have accumulated in a flu- vio-deltaic complex at a time when the sea stood at least 50 m higher than its present level (MacFadden and Webb, 1982). Fossil vertebrates collected from here constitute an assemblage representative of marine, estua- rine, freshwater and terrestrial habitats. Ma- rine and estuarine vertebrates include a wide variety of sharks, rays and bony fish, along with sea turtles, pelagic birds and marine mammals. Marine mammals collected from the phosphate mines that produced Enhy- dritherium include physeterids, ziphiids and balaenopterids among the cetaceans, and at least two species of pinnipeds, a monachine phocid and an odobenid. A walrus tusk was collected from the same general vicinity in the Fort Green mine as the paratype of E. terraenovae (Eric Kendrew, personal com- mun.).
