The fauna is dominated by terres- trial mammals although it includes several species of nearshore or estuarine fish (e.g., a myliobatid ray; black drum, J'ogonias; snook, Centropomus; and a jack, Carrangidae). Al- though the marine component of this fauna is small, it does indicate proximity to the sea at the time of deposition. Enhydritherium terraenovae must have been partially, if not totally, adapted for a marine existence based on its restricted occurrence in marine rock units or in faunas that contain marine ver- tebrates. Fossil sea otters of the genus Enhydrithe- rium (=Enhydriodon, in part) in North America have been regarded as Eurasian im- migrants (Repenning, 1976). The oldest well- documented occurrences of Enhydritherium are from approximately coeval deposits of late Miocene age in Spain (E. lluecai) and Florida (E. terraenovae).
