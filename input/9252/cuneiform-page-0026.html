The overwhelming occurrence of calcisponge, calcareous algae or bryozoan reefs in previously reported reefs have made many reef workers believe that coral reefs were absent in Late Permian time, or that rugose corals played a minor role in Late Permian reef formation (e.g., Flugel and Stanley, 1984). However, as described in this paper, Late Permian rugose coral reefs are indeed present.
