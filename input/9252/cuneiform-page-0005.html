From the viewpoint of palaeogeography, we assume that the South Kitakarni Terrane had been located in the vicinity of South China (Yangtze) or Indochina during Permian time. This palaeoposition is mainly based on several lines of palaeontological evidence, including terrestrial plants belonging to the Cathaysian flora (Kimura, 1987), and colonial corals having strong affinities to those from Indochina and South China (Minato and Kato, 1965a). Although this position contra- dicts several workers (e.g., Ishii, 1990; Tazawa, 1992), we believe the Permian strata of the South Kitakami must have been formed on a continen- tal shelf of South China or Indochina. Subse- quently, the terrane has been separated from the continent and has been slipping northeastward along major faults in Mesozoic times (Otsuki and Ehiro, 1992).
