In the many Permian organic reefs listed by Flugel and Fliigel-Kahler (1992), only a few re- ports deal with coral reefs of Late Permian age. They are: Cili-Changping (Hunan, South China: Feng et al., 1991); Thailand (Fontaine et ai., 1990); -Oman Exotics" (Oman: Searle and Gra- ham, 1982; Blendinger, 1988). The first, Cili- Changping Reef is formed by dendroid corals of latest Permian {Changhsingian) age which corre- sponds to a time just prior to rugose coral extinc- tion. The others mainly contain cerioid corals of nearly Murgabian age. The rarity of coral reefs and abundance of the other reefs is seen not merely in the Late Permian but also in the Early Permian and Pennsylvanian, as stated by many reef workers (e.g., Heckel, 1974; Fliigel and Rein- hardt, 1989).
