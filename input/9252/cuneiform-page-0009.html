Based on lithology, the Iwaizaki Limestone may be stratigraphically subdivided into eight units. Adding the underlying and overlying clastic rock beds as unit 1 and unit 10„respectively, ten units make up the whole type section (Fig. 3). No distinctive unconformity occurs at unit bound- aries. The limestone section (unit 2 to unit 9) is 155 m in total thickness. Unit 4 through unit 8 occupy the main part of the section and represent the reef and its associated facies, as described in this study.
