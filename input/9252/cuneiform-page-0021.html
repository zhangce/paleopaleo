Comparison be- tween these two Late Permian reefs reveals sev- eral differences and similarities.

The greatest difference between the Iwaizaki Reef and the Capitan Reef is the presence of an obvious constructor guild. The reef framework of the Capitan Reef lacks such large constructors as colonial corals in the Iwaizaki Reef (Fagerstrom, 1987, 1991). However, calcisponges, Tubiphytes, Archaeolithoporella, solenoporacean algae, and bryozoans are common to both reefs as bafflers and binders. Additionally, in both reefs, the solenoporacean alga Parachaetetes forms bound- stones in a relatively back-reef position of the reef framework (Babcock, 1979), although the association with Colenella is not seen in the Iwaizaki. As mentioned above, Parachaetetes joins the framework construction in the Capitan as well as in the Iwaizaki Reef. The additional sig- nificant difference between these reefs is that submarine cement is relatively rare in the Iwaizaki, while it is common not only in the Capitan but in most of Late Permian buildups.
