Calcareous algae are widely distributed, being found in the reef framework as well as in the hack-reef lagoon. Solenoporacean algae (mostly Parachaetetes) are especially abundant in both hack-reef sand area and the inner area of the reef framework behind the reef-front. As described before, these algae change their growth forms and their frequency of occurrence between the two conditions. Tubiphytes is commonly found from the reef framework to the back-reef lagoon. Archaeolithoporella is particularly abundant in the reef framework and its distribution seems to be more restricted than that of Tubtphytes In .possi- ble reef-flat areas, Archaeolithoporella is closely associated with other calcareous algae, cal- cisponges, and bryozoans to form bindstones. Dasycladacean algae (e.g., Fpimastoporella, Mizzia) are most common in the hack-reef la- goon.

The other major organisms found in the reef framework are calcisponges (inozoans, sphincto- zoans, sclerosponges) and bryozoans, which form baffiestones and bindstones. Both organisms are rare as in-situ sessile habitants in the back-reef sand area, although they become common again in the back-reef lagoon. Crinoids are commonly seen throughout the reef complex, generally as bioclasts. Fusulinids are especially abundant in back-reef sands. Pelecypods and brachiopods are generally not very abundant in the reef complex, except for shell-concentrations in the lagoon area.
