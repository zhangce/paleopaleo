Since colonial corals are the main constructor, the Iwaizaki Reef may be compared to Recent scleractinian coral reefs. As mentioned above, while cerioid and dendroid corals are distributed from the reef framework areas to the lagoonal area, they do change their growth forms in re- sponse to environmental energy input. A similar morphological distributional pattern has been recognized in Recent coral reefs of the Ryukyu Islands (Nakamori and Iryu, 1990). Here the mas- sive corals are abundant in fore-reef. While solenoporacean alga Parachaetetes, common in back-reef sand of the Iwaizaki Reef, has nodular forms which resemble the shape of Recent coral- linacean rhodoliths reported by many workers (e.g., Bosence, 1983). As a referable fossil record, Belka (1979) reported 50lenopora with concentric laminae of the tissue, under the highly turbulent subtidal environment of the Upper Permian of the Holy Cross Mountains, Poland and called it "Solenoporo rhodolites". Therefore, the environ- mental niche of solenoporacean algae in the Per- mian might correspond to that occupied by Re- cent coralline algae.
