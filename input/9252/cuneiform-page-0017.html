Biotic distribution

The main reefal part of the Iwaizaki Lime- stone (unit 5 through unit 7) includes the reef

framework, back-reef sand, and back-reef lagoon facies. These facies must have been closely asso- ciated with each other, and together form a reef complex, called here the Iwaizaki Reef. In the reconstructed reef complex (Fig. 8), the deeper fore-reef area cannot be established, because a distinctive fore-reef facies has not been recog- nized near the reef facies. Considering the thick- ness and limited lateral dimensions of the Iwaizaki Limestone, the scale of the reconstructed reef complex is thought to be relatively small.
