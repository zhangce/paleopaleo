3. Facies succession

3.1. Sand shoal facies

This facies is represented by the alternation of bioclastic grainstone and medium- to coarse- grained sandstone of unit 4. Both lithologies com- monly show trough cmss-bedding and cross- lamination (Fig. 4A), suggesting deposition under stmng wave and tide conditions. The bioclastic grainstone consists of abundant elongate fusulin- ids (e.g., Monodiexodina matsubaishi ) and abraded bioclasts of crinoids, calcisponges, bry- ozoans, and solenoporacean algae {Fig. 3), ori- ented along cross-laminations {Figs. 4A, 48).
