ABSTRACT

Field work in the Sulaiman Range of southwestern Punjab has yielded four middle Eocene vertebrate faunas with marine IIIam- mals, principally archacocete Cetacea. These are intermediate in age and bridge the temporal and morphological gap between two continental early Eocene faunas with aITIphibivus archaeocetes in Kohat and northern Punjab (Pakistan), and later marine rniddle Eocene faunas with aquatic archaeocetes and sirenians in Kutch (India) and in Mokattam and Fayum (Egypt).
