Paleogeographic and lithofacies considera- tions for the Silurian have led Berry and Boucot (in press) to conclude that the portion of the Canadian Shield between Anticosti Island and the Michigan Basin was covered by Silurian carbonate rocks prior to their removal by post-Silurian erosion.

Strata of probable early Llandovery age on both Anticosti Island and in the Michigan Basin are overlain by strata containing Virgiana, considered to be of middle Llan- dovery age. All the Silurian sequence that is pre-Virgiana-bed age in both of these areas is considered herein to be early Llandovery in age, that is, the Manitoulin Dolomite and part of the Ellis Bay Formation.

In Ontario, the Manitoulin Dolomite is a richly fossiliferous unit which Berry and Boucot (in press} consider to be of early Llandovery age.
