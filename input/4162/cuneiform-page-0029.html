At Seigas Quarry, about 10 miles northwest of St. Leonard, New Brunswick, there are excellent exposures of steeply dipping graywacke and conglomerate of early Llandovery age (area 5, Fig. 1). The Seigas Quarry area is on the northwest Hank of the Aroostook-Matapedia Carbonate Belt. Sedimentary structures in the Seigas Quarry include graded bedding, con- volute bedding, load casts, ripple marks, scour and fill, and edgewise conglomerate that suggest deposition by turbidity currents. The pebbles in the conglomerates include a variety of lithologies: mudstone, siltstone, quartzite, slate, and several kinds of ca,rbonate rocks of which ribbon limestone is one of the most abundant. The relation of the strata exposed in the Seigas Quarry to the nearby Carys Mills Formation (described below) is uncertain. According to reconnaissance mapping by David Roy and Ely Mencher {written com- mun., 1966), it is probable that the Seigas Quarry beds rest stratigraphically above a large part of the Carys Mills Formation.
