These fossiliferous rocks form the Arisaig Belt (Berry and Boucot, in press}, which may extend from the Antigonish region of Nova Scotia southwest through the Cobequids and thence out into the Bay of Fundy region, parallel to the central-source area. The rocks in the north- western portion of the belt in Antigonish and northeastern Pictou Counties {area 11, Fig. 1) make up the Beechhill Cove Formation and include dark blue-gray, argillaceous, fine- grained sandstone and siltstone, from 160 to 200 feet thick.
