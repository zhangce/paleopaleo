Such rocks consist typically of smooth, lithographic to sub- lithographic, brown to dark-brownish-gray (micritic) limestone in beds about 1 inch to 6 inches thick, with thinner interbeds of gray, shaly or silty limestone, or calcareous shale. Lamination is mainly confined to the argil.- laceous beds. The contacts between the alter- nating pure and argillaceous limestone beds are sharply defined, and their different weathering characteristics give most outcrops a strikirigly regular-banded appearance. The weathered surfaces of the purer limestone are a very clean light-gray color. Locally, beds as much as 8 inches thick consist of coarse, massive, cri- noidal limestone. This rock is brown or pinkish brown, and has a strong bituminous smell. Locally, also, there are beds of limestone conglomerate with a pink, crinoidal matrix.
