Although the actual contact of the Cabano

Formation is not exposed, in the Lac Prime area, 2000 feet of horizontal strata of the Cabano Group are exposed above vertical strata of the Quebec Group (here dated a.s of Normanskill age with Clirnacograptus bicornis, L. M. Cumming, personal commun., 1961), thus implying an unconformity between the

two units. A transition between the Cabano and Lac Raymond (=Pointe Aux Trembles)

Formations is exposed along Orient Brook, where the upper contact of the Cabano was placed by Lajoie (1961) at the appearance of Lac Raymond volcanic material.

The Cabano Formation is composed of clastic rocks, ranging from shales to boulder conglomerates. The Cabano can be subdivided into three main rock associa tions (facies): conglomerate-sandstone, shale-mudstone-sand- stone, and shale-mudstone (Lajoie and others,

1968). The distribution of the different facies

is erratic, both laterally and vertically.
