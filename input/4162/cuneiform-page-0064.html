The carbonate rocks of the platform grade eastward and southward into the strata of the Sandstone Belt. There is little reason to doubt that the carbonates of the North American platform extended from Michigan to Anticosti Island, covering the Precambrian Shield. Traces of these lower Llandovery carbonates have not been found on the Shield so far. The same reasoning should be true also for the Sandstone Belt, where no trace remains, unless it is in the present St. Lawrence river bedrock or to the south in the subsurface below Logan's Line.

The source for the terrigenous strata of the

Sandstone Belt and the Central Clastic Belt

lay in a western source area. This source area probably was massive because in Late Ordo- vician time it was responsible for the Queenston Delta, stretching from southern Quebec to the Niagara Peninsula. In Early Silurian time it was responsible for the Cabano Formation, locally containing 2000 feet of conglomerate within 6000 feet of strata. The Sandstone Belt was deposited in a brackish, shallow-water sea at the eastern edge of this western source area.
