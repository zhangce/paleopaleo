Strata assigned to the Sandstone Belt CBerry and Boucot, in press; Fig. 1) for the early Llandovery are known in a series of scattered exposures and subsurface occurrences from southwestern Virginia to eastern Ontario and adjacent New York. The rock units included by Berry and Boucot in the Sandstone Belt for the early Llandovery include the lower parts of the following formations: Clinch Sandstone, Grimsby Sandstone, Massanutten Sandstone, Shawangunk Formation, and Tuscarora For- mation. All these units consist chieHy of medium- to coarse-grained sandstone and inter- bedded siltstone. None of these rocks has yielded fossils useful for purposes of bio- stratigraphic correlation, although in places they contain eurypterids, fish, linguloid brachiopods, and fragmentary plant remains,
