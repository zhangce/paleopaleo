The regional relationship of the strata of the Smyrna Mills Formation and the limy rocks of the Carys Mills Formation type that contain early Llandovery graptolites at locality 3A is not certain. The similar age of rocks on either side of the Carys Mills-Smyrna Mills contact at localities 0 and 5 clearly indicates that the contact contains beds of basal middle Llan- dovery age. The presence of the Smyrna Mills Formation at locality 3A in contact with limy Carys Mills-type rocks containing zone 17 to 18 graptolites may be a result of intertonguing along strike at a somewhat earlier interval of time than the forming of the gradational con- tact at localities 4 and 5. Figure 4 is a schematic diagram illustrating the possible nature of this intertonguing prior to diastrophism. The present position of the rocks at localities 3A and 3B {Fig. 3) may have been achieved through faulting or through repetition by folding, whereby an intertongued section is brought to the surface in a tight fold. Certainly the area is characterized by close folding and some faulting (Pavlides and Berry, 1966, p. B 1). Thus, it ap- pears that the basal portions of the Smyrna Mills Formation, as a result of facies changes through intertonguing, trey locally be of early Llandovery age.
