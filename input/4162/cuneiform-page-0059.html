Southeast of the Arisaig Belt, Berry and Boucot {in press) recognize a belt which they designate as the Annapolis Valley Belt. The Arisaig and Annapolis Valley Belts are con- tiguous, sharing a common northeasward t-rend ing border. The lower part of the White Rock Formation of the Annapohs Valley {area 13, Fig. 1) is here assigned to the lower Llandovery. The white quartzite and interbedded dark-gray slate of the White Rock Formation are litho- logically similar to the Glencoe Brook Forma- tion of the adjacent Arisaig Belt. The White Rock Formation has not yielded fossils, but is underlain by the Halifax Formation, which contains dendroid graptolites of Trernadocian age, and is overlain by the Kentville Formation of Ludlow age which contains identifiable monograptids {Crosby, 1962).
