Graptolites of British zone 19 occur (locality 6, Fig. 3) in central New Brunswick in rocks similar to the Smyrna Mills Formation of Maine (Pavlides, 1966b, in press). These graptolites are found in the ditch on the east side of the northerly trending road at a road in- tersection about 0.6 mile north of Blowdown Settlement in the %oodstock sheet. Although the fossils are poorly preserved and distorted, thin members of the Climacograptus scalaris group and a monograptid with a curved rhabdosome and hooked thecae are present, and their presence together suggests an age in. the early part of the Silurian. Climacograptids of the C. scalaris group do not, in general, range into rocks younger than British graptolite zone 21, and the thinner members of that group are commonly found in the older part of the age range of the group. Further, monograptids of the type found at locality 6 appear initially in beds which are the approxirrete correlatives of British zone 19. Therefore, joint occurrence of the climacograptids of the C. scalaris group and the monograptid at locality 6 suggests early middle Llandovery age.
