The southern extension of the belt is based on lithologic extrapolation southward from dated lower Llandovery rocks in the Houlton-Smyrna Mills area of Maine (described above). In the Waterville area of Maine, the oldest dated Silurian rocks are in the Waterville Slate which contains graptolites of probable late Llandovery age. On the supposition that some of the Waterville Slate itself may be as old as early Llandovery or that the rocks below may be of this age, the Central Clastic Belt is pro- visionally shown as extending through this region (Fig. I). The extension. of the belt south from the Waterville area of Maine rests en- tirely on lithologic correlation based largely on assumed facies relationships.
