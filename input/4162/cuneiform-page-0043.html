Clastic rocks, which consist in most places of lenses of slate or slate and gray- wacke, occur at difkrent stratigraphic levels within the Carys Mills. Convolute layering, graded bedding, and current features char- acteristic of turbidites are locally present within the formation and may indicate a deposit of calcareous flysch (Pavlides, in press). %'ithin the Bridgewater quadrangle of Maine, the Carys Mills is estimated to be as much as 12,000 feet thick, although it thins in the western part of the quadrangle to about 1500 feet (Pavlides, 1965}. As described earlier, the upper contact of the Carys Mills is gradational with the over- lying younger rocks of the Central Clastic Belt.
