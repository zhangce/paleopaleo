The struc- ture of the area is complex, so that no section exposes the whole formation; in fact, the stratigraphic sequence within the formation has yet to be worked out. It is evident that the rocks included within the White Head were so assigned because: (1) similar varieties of very fine-grained, sublithographic limestone occur throughout the urut (though other rock types are present); and (2) fossil collections from a number of localities were all dated as Upper Ordovician (Schuchert and Cooper, 193O; Cooper and Kindle, 1936). Recent work has revealed the presence of both older and younger fossil assemblages associated with variations in gross lithology, and there are now grounds for doubt that the White Head should be regarded as a single formation. However, the name is retained here pending a better understanding of the stratigraphy.

The White Head Formation in the Perch area has yielded a rich variety of fossils includ- ing graptolites of upper Caradocian age, tri]obites of the lower middle Ashgillian, upper Ashgillian, and the Silurian, as well as a biserial graptolite within Silurian strata, and Clinton ostracodes.
