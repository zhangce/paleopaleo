A detailed description of the stratigraphy, lithology, and litho- facial relationships was given in Sanchez et aL (1991), and the following brief synopsis is drawn from it. Six distinct lithofacies were recognized, and the whole sequence of the Los Espejos Formation is interpreted as having been deposited in a shelf environment periodically affected by storms. During an initial transgressive phase, probably related to a relative rise of sea level, offshore deep-water sediments were deposited. Sporadi- cally, these sediments were affected by storms because distal tempestites are present. These sequences characterize the lower part of the Los Espejos Formation. The middle part of the unit is interpreted as deposition on a storm-dominated proximal shelf. In this regressive, progradational sequence, several small- scale transgressive to regressive cycles indicate minor sea-level fluctuations. At maximum progradation, shoreface storm de- posits were developed. The upper part of the sequence evolved to a muddy shelf with moderate storm in6uence; this may be related to a second rise in sea level or more probably to an increase of mud supply. An important regressive phase, followed by a rapid and ephemeral transgression, was proposed to explain the strong lithological and faunal change that occurs near the top of the formation (Sanchez et al., 1993).

Studies on graptolites (Cuerda, 1985), brachiopods (Benedetto et al., 1992), trilobites (Edgecombe et al., in press), and ostra- codes (Vannier et aL, in press) attribute a Wenlock-early Loch- kovian age to the Los Espejos Formation (Figure 2).

In the Cerro del Fuerte section, the Los Espejos Formation is conformably overlain by Devonian beds assigned by Furque (1979) to the El Fuerte Formation and to the Talacasto For- mation by Toro (1987), which is preferred here. The Talacasto Formation is well exposed in the Lorna de Los Piojos section, where it comprises over 160 m ofhighly fossiliferous sandstones, siltstones, and shales. The stratigraphy of the Talacasto For- mation has been previously described by Espisua (1968), Baldis (1975), and Baldis and Cane (1968). Astini (1991) has made a detailed facies analysis of this unit and interpreted it as being deposited on a mud-dominated, middle to outer shelf. He has recognized three facies: mud-dominated shelf devoid of wave action, mud wave-influenced shelf, and a condensed interval.
