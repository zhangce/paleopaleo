Discussion. â€” The main differences that separate A. modesta n. sp. from the specimens regarded as A. esch~egei Clarke, 1913, are the convexity of the right valve and the length of the posterior auricle. The total size and sculpture of the posterior auricle (more evident and regular in A. modesta than in A. eschwegei) are additional differences. Nevertheless, A. modesta and A. es- ch~egei are similar in their shell outline, ornamental pattern on the flanks, and anterior auricle, as well as in the absence of a furrow (or sinus) delimiting the anterior auricle. Height/length and length/oblique length ratios are approximately similar (see Tables 6 and 7). However, obliquity of the shells (angle n) ap- pears higher in A. modesta (values of angle a in A. modesta are not higher than 50', whereas those of A. eschwegei reach 58').

Among the other species of Actinopteria, A. migrans migrans (Barrande) from the Upper Silurian of Czechoslovakia is the closest (Korejwo and Teller, 1964). However, A. migrans mig- rans bears radial lines on both the anterior and posterior auri- cles, and a well-impressed furrow limiting the anterior auricle.
