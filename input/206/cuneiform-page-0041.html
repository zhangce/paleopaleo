A comparison between Devonian faunas in South America shows many differences. A brief analysis of the genera from the Table 11 demonstrates the following. 1) At present, the most diverse fauna is found in Bolivia. In decreasing order are Ven- ezuela, Brazil (Parana basin), Argentina (Precordillera basin), and Antarctica 2) .Nuculites, Palaeoneilo, and Actinopteria are the only genera present in all five mentioned localities. 3) The Malvinokaffric genus P/eurodapis (Melo, 1988) is found in Ven- ezuela as well as Brazil but has not been recorded from other South American localities. 4) Composition of taxonomic groups (above genus level) shows remarkable differences: nuculoids are more important in the Precordillera than in other localities. Pterioids, represented by the cosmopolitan Actinopteria, are very rare in Brazilian beds (Parana basin). 5) Each locality has taxa in common with Devonian faunas from distinct regions of the Northern Hemisphere; such genera may or may not be present in other South American localities. Except for Anthracoleda, a genus recently described from Carboniferous beds in Venezuela (Benedetto, 1980), the genera of the Precordillera are cosmo- politan and the novelties are at subgeneric or specific levels.

It is evident that these localities have a particular faunal com- position, which involves more than endemic elements. Incur- sion of some bivalves from lower latitudes by means of south- ward current-rafting could be an explanation for the occurrence of cosmopolitan genera, as has been proposed by Pojeta et al. (1976) for the Silurian genus Dualina and by Isaacson and Sa- block (1988, 1990) for brachiopod faunas. However, this sug- gestion does not explain the differences in faunal composition among the South American localities.
