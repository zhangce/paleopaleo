Environmental factors produced a dom- inance of nuculid bivalves in the northwestern basin (see Table 10), where the faunas developed in a deep-water, temporarily anoxic, fluctuating food source environment. Those of the Pre- cordillera basin flourished on a muddy, storm-dominated shelf (Sanchez et al., 1991).

Few Silurian genera of Bolivia (Table 10) are shared with both the Precordillera and the northwestern faunas of Argentina, with Nuculites the only taxon in all three regions. No nuculids other than Nuculites, Praenucula, and Palaeoneilo have been reported from Bolivia. The lack of these taxa and the presence of Actin- opteria could suggest more normal marine shelf conditions than those of northwestern Argentina.

From Devonian beds in the Parana basin of Brazil, Clarke (1913), Kozlowski (1913), Petri (1967), and Morsch (1983, 1984, 1986, 1987) reported about 15 genera, including nuculoids, solemy- aceans, modiomorphaceans, pholadomyaceans, and heterodon- tids (Carditacea). No pectinaceans have been mentioned and pterioids are quite rare (Petri, 1967; Melo, 1988) (Table 11). Bolivian Devonian sequences have 22 genera, including nucu- loids, pteriaceans, modiomorphaceans, praecardioids, phola- domyaceans, crassatellaceans, anthracosiaceans (Amnigenia), and a probable pectinacean ('?Lyriopecten) (Ulrich, 1892; Knod, 1908; Kozlowski, 1923; Branisa, 1965). This fauna is presently the most diverse known from South American Devonian se- quences. Faunal lists and descriptions of Weisbord (1926), Lid- dle et al. (1943), Bowen (1969), and Benedetto (1979) from Devonian deposits of Venezuela list approximately 18 genera, which include nuculoids, pterioids, pectinaceans, pholadomy- aceans, modiomorphaceans, and heterodontids (Table 11).

The Devonian beds of northwestern Argentina are incom- pletely known and little data are available (Feruglio, 1930). For this reason, they are not considered here.
