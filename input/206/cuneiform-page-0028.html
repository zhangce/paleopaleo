Radial orna- mentation predominates on valve flanks, whereas concentric plan more conspicuous on auricles. Radial sculpture consists of relatively coarse costae, closely packed at umbonal region (two per mm) and gradually more dispersed distally, according to enlargement of shell. Second order, progressively coarser costae intercalate between first order ones at midway of body shell. Distally, first and second order costae, as well as interspaces, are wider and only one costae per mm is found (even one costae in 3 mm in some specimens). Concentric sculpture consists of irregularly spaced, sometimes very marked rugae. Radial or- namental pattern overimposed to concentric rugae gives retic- ulate surface on body shell. Intersection of concentric and radial ridges form small nodules, which are evident in well-preserved specimens.

Ornamentation on auricle surfaces differs from that of flanks in predominance of concentric pattern. Anterior auricle shows only ill-marked, isolate, fine radial costae, as the concentric rugae dominant sculpture. In most specimens this slender radial pattern of posterior auricle not visible.
