As a result of the variations, comparison among species of the genus is hazardous when their variability range is unknown. In addition, many Malvinokafrric Devonian species are not al- ways clearly determined, as has been previously pointed by Clarke (1913, p. 179): "... all writers on this subject are doubt- less conscious that some of these "species" are phases of pres- ervation only." Identification of Silurian specimens from Bo- livia by Pojeta et al. (1976) exemplified the difficulty arising from shell shape variations. Three specimens were assigned to Nuculites sp.; they were similar to a specimen of N. pacatus Reed, illustrated by Clarke (1913, Pl. 10, fig. 23), but also rather diFerent from other conspecific specimens illustrated in the same figure (Pojeta et al., 1976, p. 18).

The specimens of N. pacatus Reed figured by Clarke (1913), Kozlowski (1923), and Branisa (1965) are similar to some of our specimens and fall within the upper half of Figure 7 (ellip- tical outline). Clarke (1913, p. 181) pointed out the ovoid outline of the margins and the "extremely thick clavicular ridges" of this species. Some of the studied material displays thick septa, although this feature is not linked to shape. Thus, it is possible to regard N. pacatus Reed as an end-member type of a greater, highly variable species, such as N. argentinum n. sp.

Species such as N. smithi Clarke and N. oblongatus Conrad, identified in Brasilian Devonian beds by Morsch (1984), are similar in shape (ovoid or elliptical) to some specimens from the Precordillera; however, they differ in the length of anterior septum, nearly as long as the maximum height in N. smithi and only extending to half height in X. oblongatus. Nuculites oblon- gatus also has been recorded from Bolivian Devonian beds by Kozlowski (1923); the specimens described by him are similar to those identified from Brazil. Both collections fall within the elliptical specimens without the projected umbo of N. argentin- um n. sp. Two specimens figured by Ulrich (1892, Pl. II, figs.

16, 17) as N. beneckei Ulrich are similar to the subtriangular ones of the Precordillera, differing only in a more medial po- sition of the umbo.
