GEOLOGIC SETTING

The bivalves described here were collected from the Los Es- pejos Formation (Silurian to Early Devonian) and the Talacasto Formation (Devonian). Both units have a wide geographical distribution in the Precordillera Mountain belt, in which almost complete Paleozoic sequences are exposed. In the present work we have selected two localities, Lorna de los Piojos and Cerro del Fuerte, near Jachal village, northeastern San Juan Province (Figure 1), where Silurian and Devonian beds are well exposed and bivalve faunas are proliic. At the former locality, the Los Espejos and Talacasto Formations are exposed but bivalves have only been found in the latter. The Cerro del Fuerte locality shows the most complete exposure of the Los Espejos Formation whereas outcrops of the Talacasto Formation are here restricted to the lowermost part of the sequences (Figure 2).
