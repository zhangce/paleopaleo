The two vertically elongated, sub- umbonal median pedal muscle scars of specimen 3516, as well as the general outline of the shell, resemble X. lens Liljedahl, 1983. However, the general outline, shell infiation, umbones, and muscle scar impressions are also very similar to Praenucula plicata Sanchez, 1989, from Silurian rocks of northwestern Ar- gentina, which was assigned to that genus because it lacks the resilifer.

The limited material from the Precordillera does not have a well-preserved hinge, and, therefore, it is not possible to know if a resilifer is present. The resilifer is a characteristic feature of Xuculoidea (Keen, 1969; Liljedahl, 1983), although McAlester (1965) did not mention it in his description of X. musculosa. Because of this problem, the assignment of this material must be made with caution. Because of the Devonian age of both the Antarctic material and the original collection from Bolivia (Ctenodonta musculosa Knod, 1908, reassigned to Xuculoidea by McAlester, 1965), the name Nuculoidea is tentatively applied to the Precordillera material.
