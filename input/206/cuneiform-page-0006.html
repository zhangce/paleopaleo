Towards the middle part of the formation (40 m depth) the ratio of sandstone to shale increases and more sandstone beds with primary sed- imentary structures are preserved. Due to a sea-level rise, the system returns to a mud-dominated shelf facies, with several fossiliferous carbonate beds. Finally, at the top of the unit, an increasing depth with a low rate of sedimentation generated a condensed interval.

Recent studies on brachiopod faunas from the Talacasto For- mation (Herrera, 1993) indicate a Lochkovian-Emsian age for this unit.
