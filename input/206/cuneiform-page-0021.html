Specimens recorded from Silurian and Devonian beds of the Precordillera show a great range of variability. Variations in- clude some features that are diagnostic at specific levels, such as shell shape or presence/absence of posterior septum. Validity of this last character was discussed by Babin (1966); his view that variations of posterior septum coexist in specimens be- longing to the same population is clearly illustrated in the Pre- cordilleran specimens. %'ith regard to shell shape, some mea- surements (Table 4) have been selected to show the variations graphically and as a method to understand their range. These variations are presented on the basis of two measurements: maximum height (Hl) as measured from umbo, and minor height (H2) as measured from a point in the posterior third of dorsal edge (Figure 6). A ratio of H2 to Hl tending to the value 1.00 represents ovate or elliptical shells; decreasing values (tend- ing to 0.60) correspond to subtriangular shells. The variation in outline includes elongate to short valves, which is expressed by the maximum height-length ratio (Hl/L ratio). Relation of both the H2/H 1 and H 1/L ratios gives the variation in range of shape of the Precordilleran material. Figure 7 shows the plotted values for Ã argentinum and N. africanus between four extreme shell outlines: oval-elongate, subtriangular-elongate, oval-short, and subtriangular-short. The low value of correlation coefficient (r = 0.13) indicates that the two ratios are not closely linked. The regression line shows a tendency towards a more triangular shape with shortening of the shell.
