Be- cause I eptodomus is a junior synonym of Schizodus and the Brazilian specimens differ from the latter, Morsch (1986) reas- signed the species capricornus to Grammysioidea on the basis of prosogyrate beaks, prominent umbones, posterior keel, me- dian depression in the Aanks, and sculpture. These features are broadly coincident with those of the Precordilleran material.

Definition of Grammysioidea Williams and Breger has been a matter of discussion. Initially, it was erected as a subgenus of Grammysia De Verneuil, by Williams and Breger, for species that either lack a radial keel or show an attenuated radial sulcus and fold. On the basis of the variability of these features among conspecific individuals, Babin (1966, 1973, 1986) discussed the validity of Grammysioidea. McAlester (1962) pointed out the difficulties concerning the definition of this genus, but he ac- cepted Grammysioidea as a valid taxon until a restudy of these genera could be made. As the Precordilleran material is inad- equate for a meaningful taxonomic discussion, it seems prudent to follow Morsch (1986) and McAlester (1962) in regarding Grammysioidea as a valid genus.

Size and shell features of the Precordilleran specimens closely resemble G. capricornus, as described by Clarke (1913) and Morsch (1986).
