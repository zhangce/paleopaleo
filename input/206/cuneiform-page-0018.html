The north- western Argentina specimens were assigned to Myoplusia Neu- mayr, 1884, on the basis of their elongate subumbonal pedal muscle impressions, originated during growth. The material from the Precordillera shows similar, but less extensive, subumbonal impressions in comparison with the total size of the shell. The presence of subumbonal pedal muscle scars is a common feature in well-preserved shells (as in Palaeoneilo cf. P fo.rbesi) but usually only in the last growth stage before death. The main difference between Myoplusia and Palaeoneilo is the presence or absence of pedal impressions left by muscle attachment dur- ing growth (McAlester, 1968, 1969). Therefore, the Precordil- leran material lies in an intermediate position between both genera, due to the median development of their subumbonal impressions.
