The fossils apparently re- mained unstudied and were returned to the Canterbury Museum too late to be included in Glaessner's 1960 monograph. Allan died in 1966 but his collection was eventually sent to Glaessner along with material collected by Mrs. A. T. Foster in the same area in 1969 (K30/f8735). This material was incorporated into a paper on Australian and New Zealand fossil decapods (Glaess- ner, 1980). Glaessner recorded four species from "Pororari"; three of these were described as new, and one was designated the type of a new genus, Pororaria.
