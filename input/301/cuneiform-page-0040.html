Carcinoplax temikoensis may be distinguished from other members of the genus in several ways. The carapace regions, even toward the lateral marlins, are moderately well dered and most are elevated above the intervening grooves as distinct domes. The three spines along the anterolateral margin are also distinct. The postorbital spine is extremely strong and is directed forward. The lateral spine is also strong, ~hereas the intervening spine is reduced in size. Finally, the anterolateral margin is reasonably short, which, at erst glance, gives the impression that it is an extension of the fronto-orbital margin. Because the species is represented by a single individual, preserving only the dorsal carapace, nothing can be said of the morphology of the venter and little can be said of the appendages.

Associated in the same concretion with the sole carapace of Carcinoplax temikoensis are several fragments of appendages that may, or may not, be referable to the species. In particular, a fragment of merus, 11 cm long and 3 cm in diameter, which is generally smooth, and two leA propodi, which are also smooth, may belong to the taxon.
