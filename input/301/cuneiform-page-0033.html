Similar structures are present on Ahachiosoma granuli- ferum but the lines defined by the swellings parallel the midline or converge slightly toward the posterior.

Applying these same criteria to PorIunites triangulum Rath- bun, the species is most appropriately assigned to Portunites and, as such, does not provide a close morphological comparison with the New Zealand forms. In fact, unequivocal references to Rhachiosoma would appear to be confined to occurrences in the lower Eocene London Clay of England (Woodward, 1871, 1873) and to New Zealand.

Four specimens of portions of chelipeds that can also be re- lated to this species have been collected from the Te Miko section. Although none is associated with carapace material at this locality, newly collected material from the Tapui Sandstone does preserve several specimens in which claws are articulated with carapaces that are undoubtedly Rhachiosoma granulifer um. Claws of this species have not been described previously.

The claw morphology that can be observed on the specimens includes the following: the palm is longer than high and massive; the merus bears a prominent spine distally and two smaller spines proximally; the propodus is quadrate in cross section, keeled, and spinose; the Angers are denticulate.
