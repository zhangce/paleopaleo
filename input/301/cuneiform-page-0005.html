However, in the early 1960's Allan, then Professor of Geology at the University of Canterbury, told a party of senior students (including the junior author) that he had made his collection at the mouth of a large sea cave, in- formation that proved sufhcient to allow his collecting site to be relocated in 1975.

Access to the decapod locality is quite straightforward. A public walkway, the Truman Track, leaves Highway 6 about a kilometer north of Bullock Creek, and ends at the southern end of a small bay with a sandy beach, at a locality shown on recent maps as Te Miko. The cave is at the northern end of the bay, immediately south of a narrow, scrub-capped point, and is readi- ly accessible for a short period only on either side of the low tide. Large collections were made here by Maxwell and S. M. Jarman in 1975 (K30/f37), by Maxwell in 1985, and by Feld- mann in 1985 and 1989 (K30/f37A).

Foster's collection, however, was made from a small bay im- mediately north of the point, and accessible through a small cave well above sea level; this collection seems to be from the same horizon exposed at the seacave. A small collection of megafossils made by Wellman (K30/f8510) contains a well- preserved crab chela and reportedly came from Perpendicular Point and from "the same beds from 1 mile to the south along coast in hard, light blue-grey, calcareous fine sandstone." This collection is shown by Wellman et al. (1973, p. 57) as coming from about 95 m above the base of the Island Sandstone, but the decapod-rich collection which is the basis of this paper is more likely to be from the underlying unit, described as "soft slightly calcareous sandstone with bands of impure limestone," 20 â€” 50 m above the base of the formation. Unfortunately, neither the upper nor the lower boundaries of the Island Sandstone are exposed nearby, so it is dificult to fix the stratigraphic position of the decapod-rich bed more precisely. It seems likely, however, that the general horizon of the decapod-rich bed is exposed at various places along the coast south of Perpendicular Point, although access is more difficult than at Te Miko.
