The outer surface of the palm is bisected longitudinally by a weakly defined keel. The upper surface is defined by two strong keels, the stronger of the two lying along the inner border. Both these keels terminate distally in prominent spines that are directed upward and distally. The inner and outer surfaces of the fixed finger bear shallow sulci, whereas the dactylus is smoothly ovoid in cross section. The occlusal surfaces bear prominent, dome- shaped denticles.
