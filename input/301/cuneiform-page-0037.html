The ischia on Pororaria eocenica broaden as they curve anteriorly from their points of articulation and they bear well-dekned longitudinal grooves along the an- terior four-fifths of their length. Sternal elements 1 and 2 are triangular and as wide posteriorly as the distance between the longitudinal grooves on the ischia of Mxp3. These elements are separated from element 3 by a sinuous groove. Sternal element 3 is as broad as the distance spanned by the ischia, is depressed axially, and has a V-shaped suture posteriorly. The lateral mar- gins of sternal element 4 are nearly straight and diverge pos- teriorly. Examination of the same features on specimens of Ova- lipes, for example O. ocel/atus (Herbst) (Rathbun, 1930, Pl. 2), con6rms the similarity and suggests that placement within this family is appropriate.
