The characters, potentially preserved on fossils, that define the Polybiinae are possession of a more-or-less equidimensional outline, an anterolateral margin with 3-5 teeth, and pereiopods that may be as long as the ehelipeds (Glaessner, 1969). These characters, however, do not serve to distinguish this subfamily from the Carcininae. Members of this subfamily lack the paddle- shaped 0th pereiopod that is developed in the Polybiinae. Un- fortunately, this limb is rarely preserved on fossils and is not present on any of the specimens of I'. eocenica; thus, placement within a subfamily must be made on other criteria.
