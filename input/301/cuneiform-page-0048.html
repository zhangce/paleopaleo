Frontal region sulcate mesially, with two spines on either side of sulcus on narrow ridges extending anteriorly onto the rostral spines; &ontal region merges with no apparent demarcation into gastric area. Orbital regions large, smooth to weakly pustulose, cylindrical anterolateral extensions terminate mesially in shal- low sulci. Mesosastric region swollen posteriorly, merging into protogastric field anteriorly and laterally; with three prominent axial bosses decreasing in size anteriorly and two smaller nodes on fianks of swollen posterior expansion. Protogastric regions smoothly domed, ornamented only by row of three nodes on either side of mesogastric margin. Urogastric region somewhat broader than mesogastric, defined anteriorly by distinct grooves extending to, but not crossing, midline, posteriorly and laterally by broadly, well-defined cervical groove that approaches but does not cross midline; ornamented by six spines, the largest and best defined lying adjacent the midline, the other two pairs broader, less distinct, located near anterolateral corners. Hepatic regions large, triangular, swollen, pustulose, attenuated antero- laterally into large, broad, anteriorly directed hepatic spines.
