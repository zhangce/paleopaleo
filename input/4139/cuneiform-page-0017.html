The local environment in which the Garnett flora grew was drier than
the environments of the coal swamps and the roof shale floras of coal seams,
but water was still readily available, i.e., the environment was mesic. The

flabelhfera var. moorei and pollen genera common in the Permian, support
this interpretation. Elias (Moore et al., 1936) described it as xeric but only

that the plants grew in a desert. The occurrence of several conifer floras in
