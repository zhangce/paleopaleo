The frond is linear, at least 10.4 cm long, up to 1.7 cm wide narrowing gradually toward the apex. The apex is rounded or possibly obcordate. The margin is entire. The rachis is up to 2 mm wide, narrows toward the apex, and is longitudinally striated. The secondary veins arise at an acute angle (45'), then bend very abruptly within 1 mm of the midrib to a nearly per- pendicular position (88'). Near the margin they curve abruptly toward the leaf apex. They attain the margm at an angle of ca. 50'. The veins either remain unbranched or branch once very close to the midrib. One vein branched halfway to the margin. Usually there are ca. 40 veins per cm of margm. The lamina is attached laterally to the midrib. The species lacks glands.
