Compansons

Callipteris flabelltfera var. moorei is distinguished from Callipteris flabel- lifera var. flabelltfera by its much longer segments, its smaller degree of com- pounding, and perhaps by the sometimes acute apices of its segments. Remy et al. (1980) describe the apices of Calitptens flabellifera var. flabelltfera as rounded, but in some of their illustrations, acute apices are visible. It is dis- tinguished from other species of Calhpteris in the same way as Calltpteris flabellifera var. flabellt fera (cf. Remy et al., 1980). Elias (Moore et al., 1936) and Andrews (1961, p.421) have compared Callipteris flabelli fera var. moorei to Mauerites gracilis Zalessky and Ginkgophyllum usevolodi Zalessky respec- tively.

Description

Callipterts flabelli fera var. moorei has one dichotomy in its main rachis. The rest of the frond is once or twice pinnately compound {Plate I, 1). Inter- calary pinnules are attached to pinnae and to the main rachis (Plate I, 1, 2, at arrows). One specimen (Andrews, 1941, p1.14, fig.5) has a segment which is at least 4.5 cm long. One fossil, which is interpreted as an isolated pinnule of Callipteris flabelli fera var. moorei, has a segment 6.5 cm in length (Plate I, 3). The common basal expansion of the rachis (Plate I, 4) is probably the point of attachment of the leaf to the stem.
