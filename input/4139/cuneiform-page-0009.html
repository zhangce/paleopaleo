Overall, the pattern in relative abundance of I ebachia is from a high
abundance at the base of the deposit to a low abundance in the middle to a
higher abundance near the top of the deposit The relative abundance is
particularly low in limestone layers due to sampling difficulties.

Unlike I ebachta, Callipteris shows no clear pattern to its distribution. lt
generally occurs m low numbers. Most specimens were found between the
depths of 20 and 90 cm where the sampled area was largest.

Cordaites was more common near the top of the section compared to the
rocks immediately beneath it. Forty-three specimens were found between
28 and 30 cm compared with 16 specimens between 31 and 60 cm. A large
but isolated concentration of Cordaites occurs at 87 cm.
