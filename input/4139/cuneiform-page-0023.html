In the Permian, the climate apparently became drier in the Euramerican
floral province. The lack of coal in Euramerica and the abundant occurrence
of evaporites in many parts of the world support this interpretation (Frakes,
1979; Zharkov, 1981). The more common occurrence of Lebachia in the
Permian suggests that it was adapted to drier conditions.

L,ebachic also possesses a number of xerophytic adaptations. It had small,
thick leaves, sunken stomata and, at least early in life, trichomes on its
leaves (Florin, 1939a).
