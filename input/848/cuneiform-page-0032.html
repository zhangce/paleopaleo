Exterior: Ventral valve widest at hinge line, length-to-width ratio about 0.6 to 0.65, height-to-width ratio about 0.2 to 0.25. Anterior margin comparatively weakly curved. Valve of larger specimens subtriangular in anterior view with Hanks gently slop- ing from beak, gently convex in lateral profile. Median sector flattened or faintly depressed in anteromedian part of the valve, forms indistinct but broad sulcus. Cardinal extremities slightly depressed, angular in young shells, subrectangular in specimens of moderate size, slightly obtuse in large specimens, always without ears. Ventral interarea moderately to steeply apsacline. Delthyrium large, open; borders include angles of more than 110'.

Dorsal valve: Beak less pointed than in ventral valve, length to width ratio about 0.6 to 0.65, height to width ratio about 0.2. Valve more or less evenly convex in lateral and anterior profiles, with faintly depressed anteromedian sector. Dorsal interarea nearly catacline, low. Notothyrium open.

Ornamentation consists of straight costae of variable size, sep- arated from each other by similarly wide furrows with subround- ed transverse sections. Costae rounded to subangular in trans- verse section, tending to fade in large specimens. However, cos- tae on the same valve tend to increase in size towards lateral and anterior margins. Concentric ornamentation consists of fine rugellae.

Ventral valve interior: Shell wall swollen along borders of delthyrium, forming distinct tooth ridges. Teeth weakly devel- oped. Delthyrial cavity deep, projecting anteriorly into a pair of short semielliptical apical plates which are fused medially, cre- ating an elevated barrier which defines the apical part of the delthyrium anteriorly; this barrier is enlarged conspicuously in larger individuals and projects somewhat into the shell cavity.

Internal valve Hoor with weak radial ribs which more or less reflect the arrangement of ribs on exterior. Large growth lines created by growth stops visible on the interior as concentric undulations.

Dorsal valve interior: The bottom of notothyrial chamber is covered by transversely striated, fairly short, broadly triangular area that is slightly concave in longitudinal section and has a prominent edge anteriorly that clearly is raised above the valve Hoor. True cardinal processes and brachiophores not developed; the brachiophore nubs are weakly developed at notothyrial an- gles.
