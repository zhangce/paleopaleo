The family Protorthidae includes genera characterized by open delthyria and nothothyria, free apical plates, and simple and inconspicuous cardinalia (basically only brachiophore nubs). The morphologic expression of the cardinalia indicates a prim- itive state of development that is additionally reflected by the overall similarity of the interior of both valves in some of the genera. Particularly in smaller specimens of SaesortIIis it may be difficult to distinguish ventral and dorsal valves; this fact is interpreted to mirror superficial shell synimetry of ancestors.

The autapomorphy of the Protorthidae is a free plate that de- fines the delthyrial cavity anteriorly and supports the ventral muscle field.
