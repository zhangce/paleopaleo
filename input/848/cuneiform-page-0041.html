Ventral valve interior: Posterior part of shell wall swollen along hinge line. Delthyrial platform short, triangular, slightly concave in transverse section, delimited anteriorly by a promi- nent transverse ridge and highly raised above the valve floor. True dental plates lacking; teeth simple and short, located at the sides of the delthyrial ridges. Diductor muscle scars probably located on the delthyrial platform. Adductor muscle scars ellip- tical, located at the border of the ridge, which defines the del- thyrial platform anteriorly. Mantle canal system and vascula me- dia not clearly impressed. Internal imprints of ribs developed only along anterior margin of valve.

Dorsal valve interior: Triangular notothyrial platform similar to but slightly smaller than delthyrial platform, developed be- tween the brachiophores, concave in transverse section, anteri- orly defined by a low and rounded transverse ridge, well-raised above the valve floor. Brachiophores small and very short, of about the same size as the teeth in the ventral valve, slightly pointed anteriorly but flattened laterally. Cardinal processes and dental sockets absent. Weak imprints on both sides of the no- tothyrial platform probably represent diductor muscle scars. Muscle field divided into two subequal portions, which represent two pairs of relatively small adductor muscle imprints. Posterior extremities of the anterior pair of imprints proceed into the field between the posterior pair. Mantle canal system not clearly rec- ognizable. Internal imprints of ribs developed only along ante- rior margin of valve.
