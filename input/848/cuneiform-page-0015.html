Subsequently, Havlicek (1970, 1977) placed the genus Ja- mesella &alcott, 1905, into the family Protorthidae on basis of the well-developed apical plate in the ventral valves. Jamesella closely recalls Saesorthis in its exterior morphology, particularly in having a well-developed sulcus in the ventral valve (which creates a conspicuous uniplicate conmussure), a sulcate ventral valve, a transversely subrectangular outline, a strongly convex dorsal valve in large specimens, and in the shape of the cardi- nalia. Saesorthis, however, is distinguished by the strikingly smaller apical plate, which, in Jamesella, clearly projects into the cavity of the valve as a tongue- or spondylium-like exten- sion. In addition, Saesorthis has a less-distinct ventral sulcus and an ornamentation consisting of finer elements than Jamesella. In that respect, the Spanish Jamesella iberica Gil Cid and Melou, 1988, which lacks a clearly extended apical plate (Gil Cid and Melou, 1988, plate 1, figure 6c), shows close resemblence to SaesortIIis, but its generic identity needs further confirmation by additional and better-preserved material.

Cooper (1976) described three new genera from the Lower Cambrian of Israel, which he assigned to the Protorthidae. These are worth detailed comparison. GlypIoria Cooper, 1976, has or- namentation similar to that of Saesorthis but is even more strongly costate and lamellose. Additionally, it is distinguished from that genus by a scabrous ornamentation of the ribs, small ears, more massive apical plates, and well-developed brachiop- hore nubs. Psiloria Cooper, 1976, is characterized by the almost smooth to weakly costellate exterior surface of the valves, which is strikingly different to the distinctly costate surface of Saesor- this. In addition, Psiloria has a less-pronounced beak in ventral view and more prominent brachiophore nubs. The third protor- thid genus of Cooper (1976), Israelaria, is superficially so sim- ilar to Saesorthis that SaesortIIis simplicissima originally was placed within that genus (Mergl, 1983). However, Israelaria dif- fers in having a strongly convex ventral valve of subquadrate outline lacking a distinct sulcus, in having acute cardinal ex- tremities with conspicuous ears, and in the obscure ribs of the umbonal region. Unlike Saesorthis, the cardinalia of Israelaria bear well-developed brachiophore nubs.

Kotuiella Andreeva, 1962, from the Lower Cambrian of Si- beria has been tentatively assigned to the Protorthidae by Cooper (1976) based on its overall morphology. However, details of the cardinalia are not known precisely. Nevertheless, the genus is easily distinguished from SaesorIhis by its smooth external sur- face and the stronger ventral sulcus and dorsal fold.
