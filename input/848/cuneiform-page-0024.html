Arrangement usually ramicostellate. Primary costae com- mence at apex, attaining up to 0.5 mm in width at anterior mar- gin; interspersed secondary costae originate both by implanta- tion and by branching; secondary costae slightly narrower than primary costae. Concentric ornamentation consists of fine round- ed rugellae that are arranged at regular intervals that evenly increase towards the anterior margin. Rugellae equally distinct on both crests of costae and bottom of interspaces. Growth la- mellae usually numerous, but only few in medium-sized valves, separated by a conspicuous distance rejecting a long period of continuous growth. However, large shells bear densely packed lamellae along periphery of valves. Number of lamellae varies in adult valves from six to 10. External surface of interareas covered by coarse growth lamellae, interspaces exhibiting fine growth lines (Figures 3.10, 3.11, 4.9).

Ventral valve interior: Shell wall swollen along borders of delthyrium, forming distinct and massive teeth ridges (Figures 3.4, 6.4, 7.3). Teeth weakly and variably developed, usually only represented by faint knobs, directed dorsally, located slightly lateral to borders of delthyrium (Figure 6.4). Delthyrial cavity deep, triangular and acute in ventral view, variable in shape, faintly striate laterally; its bottom projects anteriorly into a pair of short but massive semielliptical apical plates which are more or less fused medially; they create a barrier which defines the apical part of the delthyrium anteriorly.

Ventral muscle field faintly impressed and weakly delimited, scars progressively obscure with growing size. Adductor muscle scars usually well recognizable, narrow and elongate, usually spindle- or lance-shaped with obscure anterior borders.

Internal valve Hoor with weak radial ribs that depict the ar- rangement of first and second order costae on the external of the valve, and with a set of well-developed short ribs adjacent to the anterior and lateral margin (Figure 4.3). Concentric un- dulations reflect lines created by growth stops (Figure 3.3).

Mantle canal system pinnate, with numerous radially arranged vascular canals of more or less uniform size. They are sube- qually broad along whole length and extend from the umbonal cavity. Canals branch into numerous, slightly undulate terminal canals along periphery of valve.
