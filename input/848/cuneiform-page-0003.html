The incursion of the sea at this time marks the begin- ning of the eustatic transgressive event, which ultimately led to the establishment of widespread shallow marine conditions throughout the central High Atlas and at least throughout the "Mediterranean" part of Gondwana. These conditions and the biostratigraphy of the Cambrian in southern Morocco have been described in detail by Geyer and Landing (1995) and Geyer et al. (1995), and a summary can be found in a recent issue of this journal (Geyer and Malinky, 1997).

The Breche a Micmacca Member starts locally in the basal Middle Cambrian Hupeolenus Zone and extends in few sections into the Kymataspis Zone (Geyer, 1990b).
