Special note should be made to the resemblance of Saesorthis to Brahimorthis Havlicek, 1971, assigned to the family Boh- emiellidae Havlicek, 1977 (Havlicek, 1977). This genus is dis- tinguished by having a highly elevated, sagittally longer, and anteriorly steeply raised delthyrial platform with rather well-de- limited muscle scars, which abruptly demarcates the valve floor posteriorly. The exterior of its shell is covered with compara- tively regular, commonly parvicostellate radial ribs. Brahimor- this comes from the medial Middle Cambrian and could be re- garded as a descendent of Saesorthis. Its morphology may be regarded as a further-developed version of the patterns known from Saesorfhis. Saesorthis has weakly specified teeth and sock- et-like indentations, but these are even more faintly developed in Brahimorthis As s.tated by Havlicek (1971, 1977), Brahi morthis lacks true dental sockets, but additional materials from the type locality display weak socketlike structures similar to those in Saesorthis. Nevertheless, it should be stressed that the cardinal morphology of Brahimorthis is uncharacteristic of other members of the Family Bohemiellidae.

From the genera of the Eoorthidae %alcott, 1908, Saesorthis is distinguished by the absence of well-developed, diverging socket ridges. As a consequence, a notothyrial platform is weak- ly delimited or not developed in typical genera of the Eoorthidae like Foorthis &alcott, 1908, or Apheorthis Ulrich and Cooper, 1936. In contrast, these genera may have a pseudospondylium in the ventral valve.
