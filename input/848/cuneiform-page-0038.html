Special note should be made to the occurrence of Jamese//a perpasta which is confined to a coarse siliciclastic facies of the Jince Formation known as Milec Sandstone. That basal member is interpreted to reflect a low-stand system tract and the lower part of a biostratigraphic zone characterized by Pompeckium ku- thani (see Havlicek, 1971). Horizons bearing almost exclusively protorthid faunas with Pompeckium kuthani, Jamese//a perpasta, and J. subquadrata has been termed Orthis sandstones by Kett- ner (1923). Those horizons consist of relatively monomictic and well-sorted quartz-sands which suggest both to frequent trans- port of sediment particles and terrigenous influx which created a stenohaline milieu.
