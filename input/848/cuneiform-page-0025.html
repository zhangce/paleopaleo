Dorsal valve interior: Shell wall slightly swollen along bor- ders of notothyrium, hinge margin therefore on either edges of notothyrium extended anteroventrally into a pair of short, low, and rounded but quite distinct basal portions of the socket ridges running subparallel to hinge margin (Figure 6.3). Bottom of the notothyrial chamber formed by a fairly short and broadly tri- angular area which is slightly concave in longitudinal section and covered by transverse striae; notothyrial chamber anteriorly defined by prominent swellings, in some valves lobate anteriorly. Details of that barrier-like callosity indicate composition of two medially fused plates similar to the apical plates of the ventral valve. Growth lines on these (Figure 4.6, 4.7) are apparently parallel to major growth lines visible on the external surface of the valve. Faint scars suggest attachment sites of diductor mus- cles (Figure 6.3). True cardinal processes and brachiophores not developed; the brachiophore nubs are weakly developed at the notothyrial angles (Figure 6.1, 6.3, 6.6). Low and slender, blade- like extensions developed in larger specimens, commencing at anterior terminations of the socke-ridges (Figure 6.1, 6.3), di- verging at angles of 50' to 80' (depending on the size of the specimen). With shallow, transversely elliptical dental sockets laterally to the notothyrial angles.
