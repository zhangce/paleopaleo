Discussion.â€” The only specimen is an internal mold of a ven- tral valve which originally had a thick shell. Its outline is rough- ly subquadrate, the beak extending conspicuously, its tip being broken off. Valve highly convex in sagittal profile, more curved in the umbonal part. Lateral margins gently curved, anterior margin less so. Anterior commissure nearly rectimarginate. Me- dian sector slightly flattened, distinct sulcus absent. Cardinal ex- tremities form angle of about 110', devoid of ears. Ventral in- terarea high, apsacline, quite conspicuously bent, beak over- hanging hinge line. Ornament (as depicted on the internal mold) consisting of moderately coarse, angular costae which are sub- equal in size but slightly variable in spatial arrangement. Costae better developed towards anterior and lateral margin, but not confined to marginal crenulations. Additional costellae generated by implantation.

Unfavorable preservation of the specimen has not provided further details. Mentioned characters suggests closest resembl- ence with Bohemiella romingeri (Barrande, 1848). That species differs in several aspects such as a less-pronounced ornament with costellae of rounded rather than angular transverse section, better-developed vascula media, and probably also better-devel- oped adductor muscle tracts. In addition, the hinge margin is smooth, and fine denticles as described from B. romingeri (com- pare Havlicek, 1977) are distinctly absent.
