Notable abrasion is relatively rare at the material from the Tizi n'Tichka locality.

Specimens from limestone beds (most of the material of Sae- sorthis) are generally found as disarticulated valves that are three-dimensionally preserved. The shell generally is replaced by coarsely sparitic calcite. However, the external layer usually preserves fine details of the original ornamentation, and thin sections indicate that the shell thickness has not been notably altered by the diagenetic recrystallization.

The best-preserved specimens are now coated by delicate li- monitic laminae. Lithologic composition in thin section suggests that the preserved calcite cement, which replaced the original shell, formed within a nonferroan microspar matrix. Iron oxides/ hydroxides originated subsequently from loose fringes of ferroan calcites. In a few cases, the limonitic coatings grew to thick crusts, which can be dissolved from the matrix by organic acids. In those cases, the original morphology is not faithfully pre- served because of the accretional growth from the original outer shell surface in both directions. A special mode of preservation is to be seen at Saesorthis sp. A at the Jbel Assermo samples BS-2 and ASS-2, respectively, where the rocks suffered a complicated diagenetic history. Silicified portions which pre- serve external surfaces have not been discovered.

Except for the Tizi n Tichka locality, the isolated locality of the Pays de Skoura, and few horizons in sections of the central Anti-Atlas, Saesorthis is usually a subordinate faunal element, even in relatively "pure" bioclastic packstones and wacke- stones. The size of the valves of Saesorthis is roughly in the same size class as the sclerites of other organisms such as tri- lobites.
