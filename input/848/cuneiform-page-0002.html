INTRODUCTION

RTHID BRACHIOPODS are only a sparse constituent of early

and medial Middle Cambrian Mediterranean faunal as- semblages. Except for Bohemian faunas, they have not yet been treated in detail, and imperfect preservation in the typical clastic facies did not challenge careful study. Nevertheless, the material used for this study comes from collections of approximately 60 sections located in the High Atlas and Anti-Atlas Mountains over a maximum distance of more than 700 km. Although the material comes mainly from the west-central High Atlas and the central Anti-Atlas, it gives a representative sample of the lower Middle Cambrian orthid faunas of southern Morocco. Due the limited number of specimens, some of the brachiopods can be described only informally. However, the relatively extensive in- vestigations of our working group within the last two decades appears to exclude major new discoveries in near future.
