Cambrian Orthida previously described from Morocco include two genera based on Moroccan type material (Brahimorthis Havlicek, 1971, and the monotypic genus Saccogonum Havli- cek, 1971) and a limited number of species assigned to genera previously known from other regions.

The type species of Brahimorthis, B. antiqua Havlicek, 1971 occurs in the medial Middle Cambrian BaduIesia tenera Zone of the central Anti-Atlas. Brahimorthis pompeckiana Havli7:ek, 1977, was described subsequently from the approximately time- equivalent Jince Formation of Bohemia. Specimens tentatively assigned to B an.tiqua were reported (without figures) from the Iberian Chains of Spain (Havticek in Havlicek and losopait, 1972).

Saccogonum is believed to come from basal Upper Cambrian layers of various localities, although the precise stratigraphic range has yet to be confirmed. To prevent further confusion it should be noted that the types come from a locality traditionally regarded as situated on Algerian territory.

Another Cambrian orthacean brachiopod that was identified from Morocco was described as "Eoorthis Romingeri Barrande" by Termier and Termier (1950, plate 54, 59, figures g, 9) from probable Middle Cambrian rocks of the Moroccan Meseta. The nominal species is now assigned to the monotypic genus Boh- emiella Schuchert and Cooper, 1931. However, the Moroccan specimens reveal only external surfaces, which are insufficient for precise taxonomic determination.

Additional orthid material from various parts of Morocco has been figured by Termier and Termier (1950) as imprecise line drawings. Examination of the type material has shown that most of these specimens are poorly preserved and do not permit pre- cise determination. Indeed, some of the species are not even brachiopods. Restudy of this material did not provide significant fresh information on both morphology and taxonomic affinities. In most cases, additional material cannot be obtained from the original localities for various reasons; hence, these forms are not treated herein.
