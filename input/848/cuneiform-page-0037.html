As mentioned above, Jamesella was placed subsequently into the family Protorthidae by Havlicek (1970, 1977) on the basis of the well-developed apical plate in the ventral valves. Jame- sella, and particularly J. perpasta, closely recalls Saesorthis in its exterior morphology, especially in having a well-developed sulcus in the ventral valve (which creates a conspicuous unipli- cate connnissure), a transversely subrectangular outline, a strongly convex dorsal valve in large specimens, and in the shape of the cardinalia. Saesorthis, however, is readily distin- guished by the smaller apical plate which in Jamesella clearly projects into the cavity of the valve as a tongue- or spondylium- like extension.
