Exterior: Ventral valve widest at midlength, length to width ratio about 0.65 to 0.70, height to width ratio about 0.25 to 0.30. Lateral margins straight or slightly curved, anterior margin even- ly curved. Valve evenly convex or with a pronounced tendency to exhibit hemipyramidal transverse section across posterior por- tion; with prominent, suberect beak. Median sector of large valves slightly flattened in anterior part of the valve, forming distinct broad sulcus. Ventral interarea steeply apsacline, high, slightly concave in lateral view, almost as wide as valve and about 25 percent as high as maximum width of the valve. Del- thyrium large, open, borders including an angle of about 70'; maximum width at hinge equals about 20 percent of total width of hinge.

Dorsal valve gently convex in both transverse and longitudi- nal section; median fold inconspicuous.

Ornamentation of both valves consists of moderately coarse to relatively fine and straight costae with rounded transverse sections, best developed at median sector, tending to fade in large specimens and generally toward lateral and anterior mar- gins. Concentric ornamentation consists of up to about 5 rugae arranged in subregular intervals.

Interior of both valves largely unknown. The beak is split off in some of the ventral valves leaving an oblique lunate scar. This manner of preservation might indicate that a free apical plate extended into the cavity. The only available dorsal valve exhibits part of a very short, concave notothyrial platform, which was defined anteriorly by a massive and quite conspicu- ously raised collar-like transverse ridge. Faintly visible in places are fine radial lines, which are interpreted as suggestive of a pinnate mantle canal system.
