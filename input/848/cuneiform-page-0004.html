TAPHONOMY AND PRESERVATION

All of the well-preserved specimens come from calcareous layers or distinct limestone beds of the Breche a Micmacca Member. The name Breche a Micmacca (initially chosen for rocks at Ourika Wawrmas, central Anti-Atlas) indicates a type of lithology that bears superficial resemblance to breccias, caused by a mixture of intraclasts, numerous fossil fragments and calcite crystals. The typical "Breche a Micmacca" lithology includes bioclastic packstones and wackestones composed pri- marily of disarticulated trilobite sclerites and brachiopod valves. However, fossil content and fossil spectrum varies considerably and within short distance.

At the Tizi n Tichka locality (Figure, 1), the layer that yielded the fossils is the lowermost limestone bed within the member and was the only limestone layer exposed at that locality. Large calcite crystals, which constitute an unusually high portion of the limestone, are also documented in the apparent neomorphism of original shell material to blocky calcite.
