The specimens are hence kept under open nomenclature, although the differ- ences from the Bohemian 8 romin.geri (Barrande, 1848) are obvious: The delthyrial cavity in the Moroccan species is less extremely deep and, hence, more regularly triangular in ventral view; the interarea is moderately high and almost plane; the adductor muscle field is less clearly defined; and the delthyrial cavity does not extend strikingly beyond the hinge line in dorsal view. These characters clearly separate the Moroccan specimens from these of B. romingeri, but are regarded as within the limits of generic variability. Nevertheless, they call for an emendation of the generic diagnosis.
