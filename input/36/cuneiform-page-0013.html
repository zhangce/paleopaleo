Laub's inclusion of Palaeop- ora M'Coy, Geoporites d'Orbigny, and Pachycanalicula%entzel as junior synonyms of HeliolItes is followed here, but Helio- plasma Kettnerova, which he also synonymized, is retained as a distinct genus. Kettnerova's type material from the Lower Devonian of Bohemia of Helioplasma kolihai, the type species of that genus, has been thoroughly discussed and re-illustrated by Galle (1969). Galle's illustrations show incomplete tubule tabulae and dissepimentate plates in the coenenchymal tubules, features that strongly support the continued distinction of He- lioplasma from Heliolites.

A large number of other genera are here included in Heliolites as definite or probable synonyms. Pseudoplasmopora Bondaren- ko (1963) and Amphilites Bondarenko (1975) were referred by Bondarenko to her subfamily Pseudoplasmoporinae (=Pseu- doplasmoporidae of Hill, 1981). Both of these genera have cir- cular corallites, short spinose septa, and regular polygonal coe- nenchymal tubules with complete tabulae. Amphilites possesses no character that could distinguish it from Heliolites, while Pseu- doplasmopora differs only by having aureoles of 12 tubules around its corallites. Another similar taxon, Heliolites (Para heliolites) Tong-Dzuy Thanh (1966), which is considered a dis- tinct genus by Bondarenko, possesses round aseptate corallites, regular polygonal coenenchymal tubules, and complete corallite and tubule tabulae. Amphilites and Heliolites (Paraheliolites) should be considered junior synonyms of Heliolites, and Pseu- doplasmopora is tentatively synonymized.

Two genera included by Bondarenko in her family Stellipo- rellidae are tentatively synonymized with Heliolites here. Po- dollites Bondarenko (1971) has septal plates which may form a partial anastomosing network but may also be unconnected (see discussion of Heliolites laxus n. sp. below) Tarbaga.tailites Bon- darenko (1975) supposedly has septa which anastomose, but Bondarenko's (1975, Pl. 10, fig. 3) photographs of the type species, T. columellus Bondarenko from the Lo~er Devonian of Ka- zakhstan, are not clear and show little evidence of this.
