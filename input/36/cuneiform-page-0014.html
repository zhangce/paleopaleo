Dnestn'tes Bondarenko (1978) and Okopites Bondarenko (1978) both possess crenulate corallites with mostly complete tabulae, and polygonal coenenchymal tubules. Bondarenko ap- parently distinguished these two from one another, and from other heliolitid genera, by the former's long lamellar septa which may form an anastomosing network with the tabulae and which may have spinose tips, and the latter's septa of short lamellar bases and long spinose tips which may combine with the tabulae to form loose axial structures. These features are here considered of low value as generic discriminators and both Dnestrites and Okopites are considered synonymous with Heliolites. %hile such a step may initially seem arbitrary and appear to make Heliolites an unworkably broad grouping, it is based on observation of the degree of variation in a single species. Heliolires Iaxus n. sp. (see Figure 6.7-6.9) possesses lamellar septa that may be long and combine with the tabulae to form axial structures in some coralla or parts of coralla, but may be short or even absent in otherwise identical coralla occurring at the same locality. The more parsimonious interpretation would be to attribute this sort of difterence to normal intraspecific variation rather than to explain it by referring these coralla to diferent species or dif- ferent genera.

Vulykh (1983) revised Heliolites, erecting the genus Kolon gites for specimens identical to Heliolites in "morphotype" (the suite of basic morphologic characters), but distinguished from the latter by their apparently trabeculate wall microstructure. Although Vulykh (figs. 1, 2) provided line diagrams that show walls with a "tufted" appearance, none of his thin section pho- tographs is of a suSciently high magnification for wall micro- structure to be clearly seen and that which can be seen does not appear to represent original wall structure. For this reason and, more importantly, because many heliolitid species are not known from specimens preserved well enough for such criteria to be used, Kolongites is not accepted here as a distinct taxon.
