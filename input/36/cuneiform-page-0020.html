Some of the specimens included in the present study were described by Noble and Young (1984) as Heliolites lavieillensis Noble and Young. Heliolites lavieillensis was distinguished from H. interstinctus because its tabularium diameter was considered to be significantly smaller, but this distinction was based on Lambe's interpretation of H. interstinctus rather than on a thor- ough comparison of specimens of the two.

Although occurrences of Heliolites i ntersti nctus in other parts of North America have been noted by a number of workers, there has either been no illustration provided, or the illustration is so poor that no definite identification can be made, or the illustrations show specimens that are definitely not H. inter- stinctus. Coralla that probably belong to this species include those from the Silurian Niagaran series of Manitoulin Island described by Lambe (1899) as having corallites with diameter (outside?) ofabout 1.5 mm and delicate septa.
