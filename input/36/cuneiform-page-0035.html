Both of these species possess loosely anastomosing septa and meandroid coenenchy- mal tubules but are distinguished from S. cf. S. liljevalli by their considerably larger corallites of about 1.75 mm and 1.7 mm, respectively.

Stelhporella cf. S. liijevalli is apparently the first species of this genus to be described or illustrated from the Chaleurs Bay region. It was probably missed by previous workers because it is relatively rare and because it is restricted to the Gascons Formation of the southern Gaspe, which has not been as thor- oughly sampled as the West Point and La Vieille Formations above and below it.
