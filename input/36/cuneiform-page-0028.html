Although Rominger and Davis provided only external views and Lambe illustrated no material from this area, type material used by the two former workers along with other specimens from Michigan, Iowa, and the Louisville Lime- stone of southern Indiana were included in H. subtubulatus as defined by Stumm (1964). Stumm's illustrated coralla have ex- tremely long septa that anastomose to form axial structures, and are definitely not conspecific with H. disti nctus. Lambe s central Canadian specimens are likely to be the same as Stumm's and are not here referred to H. distinctus.

Foerste (1906) named two varieties, H subtubula.ta distans- and H. subtubulata-nucella, for specimens from the Silurian of east-central Kentucky. Although Foerste included only external views in his illustrations, his varieties were described and il- lustrated by Amsden (1949) as H. distans Foerste and H. nucella Foerste. Both species possess small corallites with diameter about 1.0 mm and about 0.7 mm (outside?), respectively, and the corallites of H. nucella are broadly spaced, but both are distin- guished from H. distinctus by their complete lack of septa and their thick tabulae.

Bolton (1981a, Pl. 11, figs. 9, 10) illustrated as Pseudop/as mopora sp. a corallum (GSC 66890) from the Jupiter Formation of Anticosti Island. This specimen is not different in any sig- nificant features from the Chaleurs Bay and Anticosti Island Heliolites distinctus and it is here included in this species.

Among the other species occurring in the Gaspe-New Bruns- wick region, Heliolites distinctus is most similar to and may in some cases be confused with H interstinctus (Li.nnaeus).
