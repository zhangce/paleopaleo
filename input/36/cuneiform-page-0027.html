Outside the Gaspe, New Brunswick region, specimens pre- viously referred to Heliolites subtubulatus from other parts of northeastern North America are probably synonymous with H. distinctus, while those from the central part of the continent should be referred to other species. Lambe (1899), Twenhofel (1928), and Bolton (1981a), all described or illustrated, as H. subtubulatus from the Silurian of Anticosti Island, specimens that possess wide-spaced corallites with diameter (outside) of 0.75-1.0 mm and elongate septa. These features are character- istic of H. distinctus, as are those of Shrock and Twenhofel's (1939) H. subtubulatus from the Silurian of northern Newfound- land.
