Although this species has been described and illustrated in numerous studies, the only thorough revision of it has been that of Lindstrom (1899). Lindstrom's concept of H. intersrinc tus is similar to that followed here, except that he illustrated some specimens (Pl. 1, figs. 31-33; Pl. 2, figs. 1, 2) that appear to possess strong septal spines. These include RM 17067 (Lind- strom, 1899, Pl. 2, figs. 1, 2), and should be referred to another species.

Lindstrom (1899) considered Heliolites decipiens (M'Coy), which he discussed and illustrated from the Silurian of Gotland and Germany, to be a variety of H. interstI'nctus. Heliolites decipiens, also described and illustrated by M'Coy (1850, 1851) from the Wenlock of the Welsh Borderlands and by Klaamann (1961) from the Wenlock of Estonia (but not H. decipens [sic] Sugiyama, 1940), has been distinguished from H interstinct.us only by its frequent lack of septa.
