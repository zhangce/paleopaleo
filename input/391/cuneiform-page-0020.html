Diagnosis. â€” Aboral cup probably medium bowl shaped; in- frabasal circlet subpentagonal; infrabasals probably not visible in lateral view; aboral cup plates thin, flat to slightly convex.

Description. â€” Aboral cup medium to large in size; probably medium bowl shaped. Aboral cup plates flat to slightly convex, thin.

Infrabasal circlet subpentagonal; probably not visible in lat- eral view. Basal plates large, hexagonal, slightly wider than high. Radial plates large, pentagonal with distal margin arcuate, wider than high. Radial facets peneplenary, narrow; separated from adjacent facets by radial processes formed by adjoining radial plates; well-developed articular ridge along half of radial facets, elongate centrally located fossa abaxial to articular ridge, adaxial to ridge laterally expanded but not depressed areas for connec- tive tissue attachment.

No anal plates in the aboral cup.

Brachials very thin, very short, wide. Two primibrachials, six or seven secundibrachials. First primibrachial narrower than second primibrachial, but neither occupies full width of radial facet.
