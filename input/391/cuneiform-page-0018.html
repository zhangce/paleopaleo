Gaulocrinus is known only from the Fort Payne Formation and the coeval New Providence Shale Member of the Borden Formation. Previously G. robustus (Wood, 1909) was known only from the Fort Payne Formation at Whites Creek Springs, Davidson County, Tennessee; G. trautscholdi (Wachsmuth and Springer, 1885) was recognized from Whites Creek Springs and from Button Mold Knob (the latter occurrence is considered erroneous herein); G. veryi (Rowley, 1903) was known only from the Fort Payne Formation in Cumberland County, Kentucky; and G. bordeni (Springer, 1920) was recognized only from the New Providence Shale Member in Clark County, Indiana.
