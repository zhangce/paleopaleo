The type 3 color pattern (six specimens) is somewhat similar to types 1 and 2 in some characteristics but differs significantly in other ways. This pattern has been recognized only in speci- mens of Pentremites robustus Lyon from the upper Chesterian lower Tar Springs Formation of Indiana. A vertical band closely borders the interradial sutures on either side (Figures 2.9, 2.10; 3.1 â€” 3.10). This type differs from types 1 and 2 in that the radial bodies have many transverse dark streaks, most of which are very thin and curving, and are nearly perpendicular to the growth lines of the radials (Figure 2.9, 2.10). It should be noted that these streaks do not form a symmetrical pattern on each radial but do on the blastoid as a whole.
