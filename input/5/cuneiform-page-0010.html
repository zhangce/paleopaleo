PREVIOUS INVESTIGATIONS

Only three previous reports have been published on blastoids with original color patterns or markings. The earliest paper (Rei- mann, 1961) describing these markings involved specimens of Hyperoblastus alveata (Reimann) from the Middle Devonian of Thedford, Ontario. Gutschick and Sprinkle (1967) observed rib- like ornamentation on Costatoblastus sappingtonensis Gutschick and Sprinkle of earliest Mississippian age from southwestern Montana. It consists of "small raised knobs or pustules that are regularly spaced on growth lines." The description and illustra- tions of these knobs or pustules suggest ornamentation related to growth lines and not to any type of color pattern.

In a later report Sprinkle (1973), described color patterns on a specimen of Pentremites conoideus Hall from the Middle Mis- sissippian of Indiana and bands of growth lines on the para- blastoid Meristoschisma which he suggested may be seasonal or yearly in nature. Also, in Meristoschisma, he describes the bands as "periodic ridgelike interruptions of the primary plate growth"
