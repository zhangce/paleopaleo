The studied fossils are derived from the Karakavak Forma- tion, which comprises the base of the Lower Devonian to Middle Carboniferous carbonate complex and is exposed on the south- ern slope of the Alai Ridge (Figure l) in the interlluve of the Sarydlcha and Kainutok Rivers. The Karakavak Formation consists of thin-layered calcarenites with abundant varying de- trital components, which occur in alternation with minor layers of limy siltstones and mudstones. Siltstones prevail in some locations. Lens-like beds as well as biohermal coral-stromato- poroid limestones and interlayers of basic effusives occur in the upper part of the formation.

Lithologic composition of the formation is rather consistent laterally. The only lithologic change is expressed in the eastward increase in the number of interlayered effusive volcanic rocks. The thickness of the formation is about 150 m. The lower boundary is tectonic in origin, as it rests upon strata of Late Carboniferous age; its upper contact is of a faulted nature, and it is overlain by Emsian to Eifelian dolomitic rocks.
