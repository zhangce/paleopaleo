Omphalocirrus sp. Linsley, 1968, from the Anderdon Lime- stone Member (Eifelian) of the Lucas Formation of the Detroit River Group, southeastern Michigan, was later relegated by Linsley (1978) to the allied genus Hypomphalocirrus Linsley, 1978, which incorporates several Middle Devonian species characterized by a subtriangular whorl profile, as opposed to the rounded whorl profile characteristic of Omphalocirrus. Both Omphalocirrus and Hypomphalocirrus were included by Linsley (1978) together with the genus Liomphalus Chapman, 1916 (which differs from both of the above genera by lack of canal- like protruberances), in the family Omphalocirridae Linsley, 1978. The presence in Pragian (middle Early Devonian) strata of two distinct members of the Omphalocirridae, O. kaukensis n. sp. and the type species Liomphalus northi (Etheridge, 1890), evokes an earlier point of origin for this family, most likely in the Lochkovian or even in the Late Silurian.
