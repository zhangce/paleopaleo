The identified fauna indicates a Pragian or Pragian to Emsian age.

The gastropods described here were collected about 80 km south of Osh on the right bank of the Kauk River, about one- third of the distance above the exposed base of the Karakavak Formation (overall thickness about 250 m). The gastropods are confined to clay interlayers occurring between limestone beds (see Figure 1). This gastropod fauna is typical of Pragian to Emsian (middle to late Early Devonian) gastropod faunas found throughout the Old World Realm (Blodgett et al., 1990), which is interpreted to have been situated in a pantropical, warm- water setting. Old World Realm genera found in the Kyrgyzstan fauna include Oriostoma, Coelocaulus, and Barroisocaulus n. gen., genera otherwise known only from other Old World Realm localities during the Pragian (Blodgett et al., 1990; Forney et al., 1981).
