A moderately diverse assemblage of well-pre- served gastropods has been found in addition to other fossil groups within carbonate deposits on the upper reaches of the Kauk River. This gastropod assemblage has much in common with the classical Koneprusy fauna of Bohemian and along with other co-occurring faunal groups is suggestive of a Pragian age for the enclosing strata (Lytochkin et al., 1993).

GEOLOGIC AND STRATIGRAPHIC SETTING

Nappe-fold structures of the southern Tien-Shan were gen- erated during late Hercynian (Carboniferous to Permian) time and were built up mainly by structural complexes of Early Si- lurian to Late Carboniferous age. In their present structure they form a packet of nappes consisting of overthrust carbonate (limestone and dolomite) units that are identified as sediments from the internal part of a carbonate shelf based upon palin- spastic reconstruction.
