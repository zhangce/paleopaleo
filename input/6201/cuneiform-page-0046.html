LATEST CRETACEOUS

Middle and late Laramide structural uplift of mountainous areas surrounding the Bighorn Basin (Bighorn, Owl Creek, Beartooth, and Pryor Moun- tains) resulted from a combination of vertical tec- tonism with or without horizontal compression and tensional (gravity) readjustment (Foose, 1971). This deformation may have been associated with the western movement of the North American plate against the Pacific plate (Blackstone, 1971).
