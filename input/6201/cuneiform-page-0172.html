The boundary between the Lower and Middle Heptodon Range-Zones is marked by the first ap- pearance of Hyopsodus powellianus at the 580m level, although the species does not become extremely abundant until the 590 or 600 m levels. The boundary between the Middle and Upper Heptodon Range- Zones is placed at the first appearance of Lambdo therium at the 670 m level. It should be noted that two specimens of Lambdotherium are known from levels below 670 m. Both specimens were found in the collection and their presence was not noted in the field. The date or locality of discovery of both specimens raises the possibility of pocket or slope contamination. In any event, Lambdotherium first appears as a dominant element of the fauna (7% abundance) at the 670m level.

Characteristic elements of the Lower Heptodon Range-Zone include Chriacus gallinae, Anacodon ursidens, and Tetonius homunculus. Faunal elements characteristic of the Middle Heptodon Range-Zone include Hyopsodus powellianus, "Bunophorus'* macropternus, Copelemur consortutus, and Absar- okius abbotti. The Upper Heptodon Range-Zone is equivalent to the Lambdotherium Zone or Bighorn Lost Cabin of all previous workers except Gingerich and Simons (1977) and Gingerich (1980). Species known only form this zone include Megalesthonyx hopsoni, Lambdotherium primaevum, Lambdother ium popoagicum, and Arnbloctonus hyaenoides. The last two species are represented by single specimens.
