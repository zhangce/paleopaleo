Obradovich, pers. comm.). During the late Tertiary the Caldwell Canyon volcanic rocks (Love, 1939) were deposited directly on the Wiggins Formation. The restored section given by McKenna and Love (1972, fig. 2A) can thus be corrected to show an even greater thickness of mid-Tertiary fill in the Bighorn Basin. Removal of this fill by the Bighorn River and its tributaries in the late Tertiary and continuing to the present must have had numerous consequences such as isostatic rebound and possibly large-scale down-slope movements associated with gravity tectonics.
