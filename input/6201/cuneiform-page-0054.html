The Absaroka Range was extensively glaciated during the Pleistocene (Breckenridge, 1975; T. H. Walsh and J. D. Love, unpublished data) as were the Bighorn and Beartooth Mountains.

At some time in the late Neogene, violent earth- quakes associated with faulting and extensive vol- canic activity in the Teton-Yellowstone National Park area resulted in massive gravity faulting of Eocene volcaniclastic rocks along the Absaroka Range scarp, adjacent to the southwest Bighorn Basin (Bown, in press; T. M. Bown and J. D. Love, unpublished data). Displaced rocks were distributed east of the Absaroka Range scarp across an area of nearly 1,500 km'.
