With the recurrence of backswamps came a changed backswamp flora. These backswamp floras were considerably more diverse than those of the Paleocene (Hickey, pers. comm., this volume) or earliest Eocene, containing many new species as well as retaining some of the old ones. The transition from middle Gray Bull to Lost

Cabin effected a more rapid and complete change on the backswamp flora than did the transition from Paleocene to Eocene.

The relationship between floral composition and environment of deposition is clearly a close one. The correlation mentioned earlier between change in bed geometry and faunal zonation suggests that ecological factors such as the ones that so clearly affect floras may also influence the mammalian fauna. With a better understanding of these ecological factors it may be possible to study their effect on faunal change and evolution.
