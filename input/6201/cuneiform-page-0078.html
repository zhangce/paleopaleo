The conclusion marked here, then, is that although transport has some influence on the plant assemblages of the lignitic strata of the Clark's Fork Basin, the observed pattern of variation reflects the heterogeneous mix of forest communities growing on point bars, levees, splays, and in the backswamps of the depositional basin. On a regional scale, the floral composition of the members of the lignitic sequence showing the greatest amount of transport, that is, the conglomerate and lacustrine members, contain floras similar in their composition, low diversity, and durability to those found in channel deposits. The paludal member has the highest diversity, probably due to its heter- ogeneous mixture of channel and channel margin sites as well as extensive backswamps. The diversity of the fluvial member is nearly as high as that of the paludal.
