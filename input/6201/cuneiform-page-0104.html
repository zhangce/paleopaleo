A new small species of Homogalax (not the larger "Systernodon") does occur in the basal Wasatchian at locality SC-40 and elsewhere (in fact Granger collected some fragmen- tary specimens of this himself). In our collections, Neoliotomus ultimus occurs only in the lower 160m of the Wasatchian section. I'elycodus ralstoni, Esthonyx spatularius, Esthonyx grangeri, Didymictis leptomylus, a new small species of Arfia, and the new small species of Hornogalax are among the more primitive species characteristic of the Sand Coulee beds.

We are not yet in a position to restrict or extend Granger's definition of the Sand Coulee subdivision of the Wasatchian, but this interval does appear to contain a fauna more primitive than that of the typical "Systernodon"-bearing Graybull beds.
