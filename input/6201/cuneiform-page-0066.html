Sandstone bodies in the fluvial sequence are lenticular, fining upward, often downcutting at the base, with trough cross-bedding. They are often massive or show soft sediment deformation in their medial part. A variety of structures including tabular cross-beds, parallel beds, and ripple marks occur near the top of sandstone bodies.
