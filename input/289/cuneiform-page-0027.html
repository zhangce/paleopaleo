The incisors and canines are single-rooted, the P1, P2, and the lower cheek teeth are double-rooted, and the P3-M3 have three roots, tvtto labial and one posterolingual (Fig. 8.4, 8.10, 8.15). The anterior teeth are single-cusped, bluntly conical, with finely ornamented, relatively thin enamel; the intermediate teeth (second, third, and, in part, fourth premolars) are elongate, tall, and robust with thick, very highly ornamented enamel and tall, continuous cingulae; and the posterior teeth are secodont, shearing against each other in a series of carnassials. The degree of enamel ornamentation, thickness, and cingulum development of the latter are somewhat between that observed in the anterior and intermediate teeth. The point of demarcation between the intermediate and posterior series falls within the fourth premo- lars, which posteriorly function with the molars and anteriorly with the P3/p3 and P2/p2.
