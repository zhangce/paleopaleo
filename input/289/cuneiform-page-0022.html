The broad, deep external auditory meatus opens be- tween the postglenoid and posterior tympanic processes of the squamosal and approaches the middle ear at almost a right angle to the sagittal plane (Fig. 5). There is a fossa at the end of the meatus to house the sigmoid process of the tympanic. The squa- mosal forms a broad horizontal shelf at the back of the temporal fossa, and its short zygomatic process articulates with the strap- like jugal at an oblique angle. The dorsal crest of the zygomatic process thins posteriorly and rotates downward to form the lat- eral margin of the nuchal crest. This edge of the squamosal is not confluent with the upper margin of the exoccipital along the back of the skull. Instead, the suture between the two lies within a deep, rugose groove (Fig. 4.4). The supraoccipital shield is slightly higher than wide and constricted medially (Fig. 4.2). Dorsal to the foramen magnum, the supraoccipital is oriented nearly vertically and bears a pair of large nuchal tubercles. A deep, rugose pit lies lateral to each tubercle. The supraoccipital flares posteriorly and dorsally above the tubercles, forming the concave inner surface of the nuchal crest. The foramen magnum is transversely wider than tall (Table 4, Fig. 4.2) and bounded by vertically oriented occipital condyles. The posterior surface of the exoccipital lateral to the condyle is slightly irregular but basically flat with a slight anterior tilt, rectangular in shape, and robustly articulated with the basioccipital and squamosal. The exoccipital bears a prominent paroccipital process that articulat- ed with the tympanic bulla (Fig. 4.3).
