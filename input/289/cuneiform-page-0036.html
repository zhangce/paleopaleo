The ramus of Pappocetus has a distinctive steplike notch on its ventral margin below the m2 and m3 lacking in Georgi- acerus (and in all other protocetids with known mandibles). The adult lower premolars of Georgiacetus and the deciduous pre- molars of Pappocetus cannot be compared directly, but small accessory cusps and strong cingulae are present in both taxa. The lower molars of Georgiacetus are more similar to those of Pappocetus than to any other known Old World protocetid, al- though lower teeth are not known for many taxa in this family (Table 3). The molars of Pappocetus are more slender relative to their length than are those of Georgiacetus (Table 7). Other differences are the carina from the protoconid to the anterolin- gual corner of the molar in Pappocetus is weaker but has a greater number of accessory cusps than in Georgiacetus, and the cristid obhqua is distinctly serrated or Quted in Pappocetus.
